## L1: PHILOSOPHIES (12)

- PHI-RB-001: Engineer Around The Person, Not The Product (Build your business around the dream customer you want to serve, not around a product you already have. If you engineer around the person, your life becomes a million times easier.)
- PHI-RB-002: The Company That Can Spend The Most To Acquire A Customer Wins (Dan Kennedy quote that Russell considers one of the most important concepts in business. If you can outspend your competition on customer acquisition, you will always win regardless of product quality or customer service.)
- PHI-RB-003: Amateurs Focus On The Front End (Your goal with the initial funnel is not to make money - it is to break even and build a list. The real money comes from back-end sales, continuity, and lifetime customer value.)
- PHI-RB-004: What You Track And Measure Will Grow (If you don't track and measure your business metrics, they will stagnate and die. The act of daily tracking alone causes growth, even without conscious changes in strategy.)
- PHI-RB-005: Don't Sell Broke People (Rule number one of choosing your dream customer: make sure they actually have money. Changing the bait to attract the right people with money can transform a business overnight.)
- PHI-RB-006: Your Job Is Not To Create Traffic, It Is To Find Where Traffic Already Is (Traffic already exists on the internet - people are already on Facebook, blogs, email lists, YouTube. Your job is to figure out where they are and divert them to you.)
- PHI-RB-007: Never Reinvent The Wheel (The game is not to reinvent the wheel. It is to find what is already working perfectly and model it. Tony Robbins teaches: if you want to become the best at something, find the best person and model them exactly.)
- PHI-RB-008: Create Addictions In Your Customers (At every level, every interaction somebody has with you, give them so much value that they need more. If you can cause an addiction with your customers where they keep coming back, that is the best thing in the world.)
- PHI-RB-009: Results Are The Key (The focus of your business must be on delivering results to your customers. Results create addicts. Results create ascension. Results are what make the entire value ladder work.)
- PHI-RB-010: The Bait Determines The Customer (If you create bait that attracts broke people, you get broke people. If you put the right bait out there, you get dream customers. The bait is what gets the right person in.)
- PHI-RB-011: Financial Freedom Is A Number, Not A Feeling (You must define your specific financial freedom number. For most people, $20K/month take-home provides an extraordinary life with everything you need. Without a number, the business will consume your life.)
- PHI-RB-012: The Entrepreneur's Job Is Strategy, Not Tactics (Your job as an entrepreneur is not to learn SEO, PPC, or how to run Facebook ads. Your job is to know exactly who your customer is and where they are. You can hire geniuses to do the tactics for pennies on the dollar.)

## L2: MENTAL MODELS (11)

- MM-RB-001: The Secret Formula - Who, Where, Bait, Result (Four questions that sum up the entire DotCom Secrets book: 1) Who is your dream customer? 2) Where are they congregating? 3) What bait will attract them? 4) What result/destination will you take them to?)
- MM-RB-002: The Congregation Principle (People congregate together based on beliefs, values, and interests - just like churches. The internet enables congregations around every niche. Find where your target market congregates and throw your bait into that pond.)
- MM-RB-003: Three Types of Traffic (All traffic falls into three categories: 1) Traffic You Control - paid ads you can direct; 2) Traffic You Don't Control - organic/random visitors; 3) Traffic You Own - your email list. The goal of types 1 and 2 is always to convert to type 3.)
- MM-RB-004: The Value Ladder Mental Model (Customers enter at the bottom with low-cost items, receive value, and naturally want to ascend to higher levels. Each level provides more value at a higher price. Like sushi - you eat, love it, want more, want classes, want VIP experiences.)
- MM-RB-005: The Self-Liquidating Offer (SLO) (Your initial funnel's goal is to break even - this is called a self-liquidating offer. If your front-end funnel breaks even, you acquire customers for free. Then all back-end revenue is pure profit.)
- MM-RB-006: Direct vs Indirect Competitors (Direct competitors sell identical products to yours. Indirect competitors sell different products to the same target market. For affiliate/email partnerships, indirect competitors are often better because there is no conflict.)
- MM-RB-007: The KPI Dashboard Model (Key Performance Indicators must be tracked daily and visibly. Russell's whiteboard system: products on Y-axis, days on X-axis, daily revenue tracked, weekly totals, monthly totals. The act of visible tracking drives growth.)
- MM-RB-008: The Reverse Engineering Mindset (Before building anything, reverse engineer what is already working. Buy competitors' products. Transcribe their sales videos. Map their upsells, downsells, price points, traffic sources, ad copy, and landing pages.)
- MM-RB-009: Each Funnel Is A Value Ladder In Itself (You don't have to bring someone in at the bottom and ascend them. Each funnel type - tripwire, webinar, high ticket - contains its own mini value ladder. The sales process within each funnel provides the value needed to justify the price.)
- MM-RB-010: The Accountability Partner Model (Find someone you don't already know. Write each other checks for a meaningful amount. Every two weeks, declare specific tangible goals. If you fail to accomplish them, the check gets cashed. Creates massive urgency and execution.)
- MM-RB-011: The Raise Model (How can I get myself a raise today? Each new traffic source you add is a permanent raise. Find a site that gets you 100 profitable leads per day, add it, find another - your income compounds with each new source.)

## L3: HEURISTICS (22)

- HEUR-RB-001: $20K/Month Is Financial Freedom (Russell's benchmark: $20K/month take-home provides a $2M house at $12K/month payment, a Tesla at $1.2K/month, $1K debt elimination, $2K vacations, $5K misc. Puts $10K/month in savings. More than most people need.)
- HEUR-RB-002: 100 Clicks Per Day Formula (100 clicks/day to front-end funnel = 3,000 clicks/month. At 5% conversion = 150 buyers. 30% ascend to continuity = 50 new members. From 150 buyers, 20 apply for high-ticket. Result: $20K/month.)
- HEUR-RB-003: 5% Front-End Conversion Rate (For free-plus-shipping or low-ticket tripwire funnels, about 5% of traffic that hits the page will buy the front-end offer.)
- HEUR-RB-004: 30% Buyer-To-Continuity Ascension Rate (Of people who buy your front-end funnel, about 30% can be gotten into a continuity/membership program.)
- HEUR-RB-005: 25% Monthly Churn Budget (Budget for approximately 25% of continuity members canceling each month. Need to add at least 25% new members monthly to maintain the base.)
- HEUR-RB-006: 1-in-10 High-Ticket Close Rate (If you butcher the sales process completely, worst case scenario you should close at least 1 out of 10 people who apply for your high-end coaching/program.)
- HEUR-RB-007: $50/Month Continuity Price Point (For calculation purposes, $50/month continuity membership. Need 200 members at $50 = $10K/month. Only need to add 50 new members per month to sustain.)
- HEUR-RB-008: $5,000 Back-End Price Point (For back-end high-ticket offer, $5,000 is a workable price point. Need only 2 sales per month to hit $10K from back-end.)
- HEUR-RB-009: 20 Applications For 2 High-Ticket Sales (At 10% close rate, need 20 applications to close 2 sales at $5,000 each = $10K/month from back-end.)
- HEUR-RB-010: Webinar Registration Cost $1-$3 (For paid webinar registration traffic, expect to spend between $1 and $3 to get someone to register for a webinar.)
- HEUR-RB-011: One-Third Webinar Show-Up Rate (Of people who register for a webinar, approximately one-third will show up live.)
- HEUR-RB-012: 10-15% Live Webinar Close Rate (On a live webinar, expect to close anywhere from 10% to 15% of attendees on a $1,000 product.)
- HEUR-RB-013: Three Funnel Price Tiers (Tripwire: $0 free-plus-shipping to $20. Perfect Webinar: $300 to $2,000-$3,000. High Ticket: $2,000-$3,000 up to unlimited.)
- HEUR-RB-014: $2-3K Credit Card Psychological Threshold (Most people will not pull out a credit card and call their bank to increase limits above $2-3K in a group selling situation. Above that threshold requires one-on-one phone sales.)
- HEUR-RB-015: Two Unknown Variables Maximum Rule (When reverse engineering a competitor's funnel, if you cannot find at least 3 of the 5 elements - demographics, offer, landing page, traffic source, ad copy - do not model that funnel. Maximum 2 unknowns allowed.)
- HEUR-RB-016: Test On 3 Sites, Not 1,000 (When buying banner ads, test on exactly 3 sites you have carefully selected, not through a network of 1,000 sites. One site gives you no data on why it failed. Three gives you enough signal to optimize.)
- HEUR-RB-017: Ad Running Duration = Proof Of Success (If a competitor's ad has been running for 100+ days, it is working. If it disappears within a week, it failed. Use ad duration to validate which ads and sites to model.)
- HEUR-RB-018: 100% Commission For List Building (When starting with no list, give affiliates 100% commission on the front-end. You get the customer and their email. 217 subscribers from one affiliate deal was the beginning of Russell's entire business.)
- HEUR-RB-019: Email Is Fastest Traffic Test (Email solo ads give results within hours, sometimes by 10 AM. Banner ads take longer with more variables. For fastest funnel validation, start with email traffic.)
- HEUR-RB-020: $100K/Day Benchmark For Top ClickBank Offers (Top ClickBank offers in competitive markets like weight loss do 1,000-3,000 sales per day. At $40 average customer value, that equals $40K-$120K per day.)
- HEUR-RB-021: Headline Can Change Entire Campaign (Testing different email subject lines showed one headline getting 6x more clicks than others. Moving the winning headline throughout the entire funnel - landing page, sales page - lifts all conversion points.)
- HEUR-RB-022: Two-To-Three Tries To Get A Funnel Right (If a funnel fails on the first attempt, look at each metric individually - squeeze page, sales page, upsells. Usually one specific piece is broken. Within 2-3 iterations of fixing the broken piece, the funnel works.)

## L4: FRAMEWORKS (17)

- FW-RB-001: The Secret Formula Framework (4 questions that define every business: 1) WHO is your dream customer? 2) WHERE are they congregating? 3) What BAIT will attract them? 4) What RESULT will you take them to? Everything in the DotCom Secrets system flows from answering these.)
- FW-RB-002: The Value Ladder Structure (Bottom: Free/Low-ticket lead magnets. Middle: Medium-ticket products $300-$3,000. Top: High-ticket $5,000-$100,000+. At each level, provide enough value that the customer wants to ascend. Back-end funds the business.)
- FW-RB-003: The $20K/Month Reverse Engineering Formula (Step 1: Define lifestyle cost = ~$20K. Step 2: Split $10K continuity + $10K back-end. Step 3: $50/month x 200 members = $10K continuity. Step 4: $5K x 2 sales = $10K back-end. Step 5: Need 150 buyers/month. Step 6: At 5% conversion = 3,000 clicks/month = 100 clicks/day.)
- FW-RB-004: Three Core Funnel Types (1. Tripwire Funnel - low ticket $0-$20, free-plus-shipping, designed to acquire customers cheaply. 2. Perfect Webinar Funnel - mid ticket $300-$3,000, 60-90 minute presentation provides value then sells. 3. High Ticket Funnel - $5K+, application process followed by phone sales.)
- FW-RB-005: The Traffic Trifecta Framework (Traffic You Control: paid ads on Facebook, Google, email buys - send to squeeze page. Traffic You Don't Control: SEO, social shares, PR - send to blog with opt-in. Traffic You Own: your email list - send wherever you want. Goal: convert all traffic to traffic you own.)
- FW-RB-006: The Five Reverse Engineering Elements (When funnel hacking, find these 5 things about competitors: 1) Demographics - who are they selling to? 2) Offer - what product, price point, bundle? 3) Landing Page - what does it look like, headline, design? 4) Traffic Source - where are they buying ads? 5) Ad Copy - what headlines, images, text?)
- FW-RB-007: The Funnel Revenue Model (Point of Sale funnel breaks even = SLO. Revenue then comes from three sources: 1) Continuity - monthly recurring billing. 2) Back-end Sales - high-ticket coaching/programs. 3) Bonuses - ad hoc email promotions for free money.)
- FW-RB-008: The Tripwire Funnel Structure (Step 1: Free or low-cost offer to get dream customer in the door. Step 2: Cover costs through shipping or small charge. Step 3: Upsell sequence behind the funnel to make it profitable. Step 4: Build buyer list for future ascension. Examples: DotCom Secrets book, credit card knife, cufflinks-to-suits.)
- FW-RB-009: The Perfect Webinar Funnel Structure (Step 1: Drive traffic to registration page at $1-$3 per registrant. Step 2: Mon-Wed drive traffic, Thursday do live webinar. Step 3: 60-90 minute presentation following Perfect Webinar Script. Step 4: 10-15% close rate on live. Step 5: Follow-up email sequence converts additional sales over next week.)
- FW-RB-010: The High Ticket Funnel Structure (Step 1: Video testimonial or case study page. Step 2: Application button for interested prospects. Step 3: Application form to qualify prospects. Step 4: Phone call to close the sale. For products over $2-3K that require one-on-one selling.)
- FW-RB-011: The Banner Ad Direct Buy Framework (Step 1: Use SimilarWeb/AdBeat to find competitor's traffic sources. Step 2: Identify 10 potential sites. Step 3: Contact all 10 via their "Advertise" footer link. Step 4: Negotiate using competing offers. Step 5: Select best 3 sites. Step 6: Split budget across 3 sites. Step 7: Test and optimize.)
- FW-RB-012: The Daily Revenue Tracking Board (Whiteboard with products on Y-axis and weekdays on X-axis. Write daily revenue for each product. Weekly totals on right side. Monthly totals at bottom. Track both gross revenue AND net profit. Visible daily tracking drives growth.)
- FW-RB-013: The Dream Customer Profile Framework (Step 1: Define who you want to serve. Step 2: Find pictures of them. Step 3: Write detailed profiles. Step 4: Ensure they have money. Step 5: Create bait specifically designed to attract THAT person. Step 6: Repel the people you don't want to serve.)
- FW-RB-014: The Five Distribution Channels (Primary traffic channels ranked in order of focus: 1) Email lists - solo ads and affiliate emails. 2) Facebook - fan page targeting and Facebook ads. 3) Banner ads - direct buy on specific sites. 4) YouTube ads - pre-roll ads on niche videos. 5) Podcast sponsorships - ads in niche podcasts.)
- FW-RB-015: The Funnel Diagnostic Framework (When a funnel flops, examine each metric individually: squeeze page conversion, front-end offer conversion, OTO1 conversion, OTO2 conversion, OTO3 conversion. Find the ONE broken piece. Fix only that piece. Everything else may be working perfectly.)
- FW-RB-016: The Cufflinks-To-Suits Strategy (Find a low-cost item that only your dream customer would want. Give it away free-plus-shipping. Build a massive buyer list of qualified customers. Then sell them your expensive core product. "Not everybody who wears suits has cufflinks, but everybody who buys cufflinks wears suits.")
- FW-RB-017: The Competitor Research Stack (Layer 1: ClickBank Marketplace - find top-selling products in your niche sorted by gravity/sales. Layer 2: SimilarWeb.com - free traffic analysis showing sources, demographics, referring sites. Layer 3: AdBeat.com - paid tool showing exact ads, placements, duration, networks. Layer 4: Buy competitor products - see upsells, downsells, email sequences firsthand.)

## L5: METHODOLOGIES (10)

- MET-RB-001: The Funnel Hacking Process (Step 1: Identify direct and indirect competitors. Step 2: Find them on ClickBank or Google. Step 3: Buy their products - go through entire funnel. Step 4: Screenshot every page. Step 5: Transcribe their sales videos. Step 6: Document upsells, downsells, price points. Step 7: Use SimilarWeb to find traffic sources. Step 8: Use AdBeat to find exact ads and placements. Step 9: Model the offer, landing page, and traffic sources. Step 10: Launch with your version on the same traffic sources.)
- MET-RB-002: The Email Traffic Launch Method (Step 1: Identify indirect competitors with email lists. Step 2: Contact list owners or list brokers. Step 3: Negotiate a solo ad buy ($500-$5,000). Step 4: Write multiple subject line variations. Step 5: Send the email - results come within hours. Step 6: Analyze which subject line performed best. Step 7: Take winning headline and apply it throughout the entire funnel. Step 8: Repeat with larger buys if profitable.)
- MET-RB-003: The Accountability Partner Setup (Step 1: Find someone you don't already know. Step 2: Each write a check for a meaningful amount to each other. Step 3: Set up biweekly calls. Step 4: Declare specific, tangible, measurable goals for next two weeks. Step 5: On the call, report results. Step 6: If goals not met, partner cashes your check. Step 7: Set new goals for next two weeks.)
- MET-RB-004: The Value Ladder Design Process (Step 1: Define your dream customer. Step 2: Determine the highest-value result you can deliver. Step 3: Work backwards - what medium-ticket product leads there? Step 4: What low-ticket product leads to the medium? Step 5: What free bait brings them in? Step 6: At each level, ensure enough value is delivered to create desire for the next level.)
- MET-RB-005: The SimilarWeb Research Method (Step 1: Go to similarweb.com/website/[competitor URL]. Step 2: Check traffic breakdown - direct, referral, SEO, social, email, display. Step 3: See geographic distribution of customers. Step 4: Find top referring sites. Step 5: See where visitors go after the site. Step 6: Check search keywords. Step 7: See social traffic breakdown. Step 8: Click "Show Ads" to see actual banner ad creative.)
- MET-RB-006: The Headline Optimization Cascade (Step 1: Send email with 4 different subject lines rotated across the list. Step 2: Measure click-through rates on each. Step 3: Identify the winner - it may have 6x more clicks. Step 4: Use the winning headline as your squeeze page headline. Step 5: Adapt it for your sales page headline. Step 6: Create banner ads using the same angle. Result: One test optimizes the entire funnel.)
- MET-RB-007: The Funnel Flop Diagnostic Process (Step 1: Drive traffic and collect data on every funnel step. Step 2: Document conversion at each point - squeeze page %, front-end %, OTO1 %, OTO2 %, OTO3 %. Step 3: Compare each to benchmarks. Step 4: Find the ONE metric that is broken. Step 5: Fix ONLY that piece. Step 6: Re-test with new traffic. Step 7: Repeat until all metrics hit benchmarks. Usually 2-3 iterations.)
- MET-RB-008: The Tripwire Bait Creation Method (Step 1: Identify your dream customer avatar. Step 2: Ask: what small item would ONLY my dream customer want? Step 3: Source or create that item cheaply. Step 4: Offer it free-plus-shipping or at $7-$10. Step 5: Design the offer to repel non-dream customers. Step 6: Build buyer list from purchasers. Step 7: Ascend buyers through value ladder. Examples: 108 Split Test book attracted marketers, credit card knife attracted survivalists, cufflinks attracted suit-wearers.)
- MET-RB-009: The List Broker Discovery Method (Step 1: Google "[your market] advertising email" or "[your market] list broker". Step 2: Check competitors' referring sites for email list owners. Step 3: Key brokers by market - BizOp: Dagen Smith/Home Business Agency; Personal Development/Health/Survival: Brian Littman/dedicatedemails.com; Financial/News: Jim Whalen Agency. Step 4: Contact broker, describe your offer and target audience. Step 5: Start with small test buy. Step 6: Scale winners.)
- MET-RB-010: The Weekly Webinar Machine (Step 1: Build webinar registration page. Step 2: Monday-Wednesday drive traffic to registration at $1-$3/registrant. Step 3: Thursday do live webinar using Perfect Webinar Script. Step 4: Present 60-90 minutes of value, then make offer. Step 5: Close 10-15% of attendees live. Step 6: Run follow-up email sequence Thu-Sun. Step 7: Track weekly revenue growth. Step 8: Repeat every single week. Example: Liz Benny doing $40K-$50K/week with this system.)
