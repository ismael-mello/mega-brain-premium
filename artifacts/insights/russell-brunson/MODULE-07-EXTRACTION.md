## L1: PHILOSOPHIES (12)

- PHI-RB-005: Suspend Boredom Until A Million (Use your creativity on different ways to get people INTO the presentation, not on creating new presentations - suspend the urge to create something new until the current one hits $1M)

- PHI-RB-007: Take The Play On The Road (Instead of constantly creating new products/launches, take your one converting offer and promote it through every available channel - like Hollywood taking a movie on the media circuit)
- PHI-RB-008: Business Is Just Relationships (When all is said and done, business is just relationships - who do you like, who likes you - everything from JV deals to Facebook account reps comes down to relationships)

## L2: MENTAL MODELS (10)

- MM-RB-001: The One Thing Model (Every webinar must focus on ONE thing you are trying to get someone to believe - if they believe that one thing, they will give you money; trying to convince of more than one thing almost always bombs)
- MM-RB-002: Three False Belief Patterns (Identify the three false beliefs keeping your audience from buying - smash each one and rebuild it with a new empowering belief; when all three are destroyed, the prospect has no legs to stand on)
- MM-RB-003: The Trial Close Cascade (From the first slide to the last, embed constant yes-or-no questions where the answer is obviously yes - get them nodding a thousand times before you ever ask for money)
- MM-RB-004: The Mary Ellen Tribby Model (Your business is like a smash-hit play in Boise, Idaho - instead of writing a new play, take the existing play on the road to Chicago, LA, and New York to scale from $7M to $80M)
- MM-RB-005: Pre-Frame Controls Everything (How someone is introduced to you and your offer determines whether they buy - control the pre-frame through funnel pages, indoctrination videos, and intro sequences so they arrive predisposed to purchase)
- MM-RB-006: Objects In Motion Stay In Motion (Human beings who start buying tend to keep buying - get someone to purchase something small or enter a credit card for a free trial BEFORE the webinar so the purchasing momentum is already started)
- MM-RB-007: Urgency And Scarcity Drive Action (Without urgency and scarcity, half of prospects will never move - approximately one third of all sales come in the last 6 hours before an offer closes)
- MM-RB-008: Ad Fatigue And Re-Niching (After showing the same registration page to a market repeatedly, you get ad fatigue and costs rise from $3-5 to $10-15 per registration - the solution is to re-niche the front end page to speak to different audiences while keeping the same webinar)
- MM-RB-009: Comment Moderation As Conversion Lever (On Facebook Live replays, the comments people read while watching directly influence whether they buy - deleting 700 negative comments cut cost of acquisition from $90 to $50)
- MM-RB-010: Export Questions, Improve Presentation (After every live webinar, export all Q&A questions from GoToWebinar, read every single one, identify stuck points, tweak slides accordingly - this is how you go from $30K to $120K on the same webinar)

## L4: FRAMEWORKS (17)

- FW-RB-001: The Perfect Webinar Structure (Three-part framework: 1) Introduction - grab attention, bold promise, hook to the end, qualify yourself, future pace; 2) Content - one thing + three secrets that smash false beliefs; 3) Close - the stack + closes + 30-min countdown Q&A)
- FW-RB-002: The Stack (Armand Morin's closing technique: present offer component by component, restacking ALL previous components each time before adding the next one - so when you reveal the price, they associate it with EVERYTHING, not just the last bonus shown)
- FW-RB-003: The Perfect Webinar Funnel Sequence (Registration Page > Thank You Page with origin story video + upsell > Indoctrination Video 1 > Indoctrination Video 2 > Indoctrination Video 3 > Live Webinar > Encore Replay > Online Replay + Cliff Notes > Cart Close)
- FW-RB-004: The Introduction Framework (5 components in order: 1) High-energy looping intro 5-10 minutes before start; 2) Bold promise with measurable ruler; 3) Hook to the end with free gift; 4) Qualify yourself with social proof; 5) Future pace what life will be like)
- FW-RB-005: The Three Secrets Content Structure (Each secret targets one false belief pattern: Secret 1 - smash and rebuild belief about difficulty; Secret 2 - smash and rebuild belief about time/complexity; Secret 3 - smash and rebuild the last remaining excuse; thereby proving the One Thing is true)
- FW-RB-006: The Permission Transition (After content, say "Let me ask you guys a question" > acknowledge the experience > "How many of you would be interested if I had a way to continue?" > "Do you mind if I take a minute to share?" > wait for explicit yes > then begin the stack)
- FW-RB-007: The If-All Close (After stacking total value: "If ALL this did was [result 1], would it be worth [total value]?" YES. "If ALL this did was [result 2]?" YES. "If ALL this did was [result 3]?" YES. Three yeses at full price = they cannot refuse the discounted real price)
- FW-RB-008: The Two Choices Close (Present only 2 options: Option 1 - do nothing and miss the risk-free opportunity; Option 2 - make this tiny investment for massive value. Frame it so one option looks foolish and the other is obviously correct)
- FW-RB-009: The Perfect Webinar Email Sequence (Mon: JV partner promotes registration; Tue: follow-up email; Wed AM: reminder email; Wed 1hr before: "Starting Now" notification; Thu: "Encore happening Friday, re-register"; Fri AM: reminder; Fri: encore replay; Sat: replay + cliff notes; Sun AM: replay; Sun 6hrs before: final urgency close)
- FW-RB-010: The Perfect Webinar Hack / Facebook Live Version (Write one thing on whiteboard; write 3 secrets on paper; cover each with paper flaps; go live on Facebook; deliver 26-min presentation tearing off reveals; no slides needed; can be boosted as ad afterwards for perpetual sales)
- FW-RB-011: The Dream 100 Strategy For Webinar Traffic (1) Identify 100+ people with email lists in your market; 2) Build genuine relationships before asking for anything; 3) Comment on their posts, hire them for consulting, provide value; 4) Once you have webinar stats, approach with numbers; 5) Each JV deal = $100K-$1M potential)
- FW-RB-012: Registration Page Formula (Headline = One Thing framed as "How to [desire] without [pain]"; include 3 secrets as bullet points; use curiosity-heavy language they cannot answer in their head; image should be confusing/curiosity-inducing; NO video on registration page)
- FW-RB-013: Thank You Page Monetization (After registration: 1) Video re-exciting them about webinar; 2) Date/time reminder; 3) Sell something - free trial, $27 product, or book to cover ad costs and start purchase momentum; 4) Re-registration link as backup)
- FW-RB-014: The GoToWebinar Title Hack (10-15 minutes before webinar starts, log into GoToWebinar and change the title to "**STARTING NOW** [Original Title]" - GoToWebinar emails all registrants about the title change, creating a last-minute show-up surge)
- FW-RB-015: The Indoctrination Video Series (3 pre-webinar videos: Video 1 - Anatomy/overview of the result; Video 2 - How to design/plan; Video 3 - How to build/implement quickly. Each page also reminds about webinar date, free trial offer, and 3 secrets)
- FW-RB-016: Facebook Audience Insights Targeting (Use Facebook Audience Insights to type in interests, discover demographics, age, gender, relationship status, related page likes; save audiences; split by age groups; run targeted ads to each segment)
- FW-RB-017: The Universal Perfect Webinar Application (The PW framework works across all delivery mechanisms: 1) Email sequences - email 1 is origin story, 2 is new opportunity, 3 is internal belief, 4 is external belief, 5 is the stack; 2) Product launches - video 1-4 follow same structure; 3) Stage selling; 4) Facebook Live)

## L5: METHODOLOGIES (10)

- MET-RB-001: How To Identify Your Three False Beliefs (Step 1: Define your One Thing - the single belief they must hold to buy; Step 2: Fill in "How to [desire] without [pain]"; Step 3: Think about the last 10 times your market tried to solve this problem and failed; Step 4: Identify the 3 core reasons they failed - usually tied to time, money, and one other common pain point; Step 5: Structure each as a "secret" that smashes then rebuilds the belief)
- MET-RB-002: How To Build A Perfect Webinar From Scratch In One Day (Step 1: Print out the Perfect Webinar script; Step 2: Open PowerPoint; Step 3: Define your One Thing and three false beliefs; Step 4: Fill in the blanks of the script for introduction, 3 secrets, and the stack; Step 5: Add trial closes after every statement and testimonial; Step 6: Build the stack with component-by-component restacking; Step 7: Add if-all statements, two choices close, and other closes; Step 8: Create 30-min Q&A countdown slide with pre-loaded questions)
- MET-RB-003: How To Optimize A Live Webinar Iteratively (Step 1: Do the webinar live; Step 2: Immediately after, export ALL Q&A questions from GoToWebinar; Step 3: Read every single question to identify where people got stuck; Step 4: Go back to slides and tweak - add case studies, change order, address objections; Step 5: Do the webinar again within days; Step 6: Repeat this cycle for every live performance; Result: Russell went from $30K to $120K to $150K through this exact iteration process)
- MET-RB-004: How To Execute The Perfect Webinar Hack On Facebook Live (Step 1: Sit in front of a whiteboard; Step 2: Write your One Thing as the headline; Step 3: Write 3 secrets below, cover each with paper; Step 4: Write your offer/stack, cover with paper; Step 5: Hit go on Facebook Live and/or Periscope; Step 6: Deliver the presentation pulling off paper reveals; Step 7: Stack the offer at the end; Step 8: Boost the video as a Facebook ad for ongoing sales; Total time: approximately 26 minutes)
- MET-RB-005: How To Run The Wednesday/Friday Webinar Cadence (Step 1: Monday - JV partner or Facebook ads promote registration page; Step 2: Tuesday - follow-up promotion; Step 3: Wednesday morning - reminder email + GoToWebinar title hack 10 min before; Step 4: Wednesday - do webinar LIVE; Step 5: Thursday - promote Friday encore replay; Step 6: Friday - replay Wednesday recording through GoToWebinar with staff answering Q&A live; Step 7: Saturday - send replay link + cliff notes PDF; Step 8: Sunday - replay + final 6-hour urgency close at midnight)
- MET-RB-006: How To Build Your Dream 100 List (Step 1: Go to Facebook and start liking pages in your market; Step 2: Search ClickBank for products selling in similar spaces; Step 3: Search Amazon for authors and products; Step 4: Google to find bloggers, podcasters, and influencers; Step 5: Identify secondary adjacent markets whose audiences overlap; Step 6: Build Excel spreadsheet with names, platforms, list sizes; Step 7: Start engaging immediately - comment on posts, follow on social, provide value; Step 8: When ready, approach with stats and conversion data)
- MET-RB-007: How To Re-Niche Your Front End For Scale (Step 1: Identify that your primary audience is getting fatigued with your registration page; Step 2: Identify secondary audiences who would benefit from the same webinar but need a different hook; Step 3: Create new registration pages with different headlines, images, and angles targeting each sub-audience; Step 4: Keep the actual webinar identical; Step 5: Add a "Who does this work for?" slide showing multiple market examples; Step 6: Run separate Facebook ad campaigns to each re-niched registration page)
- MET-RB-008: How To Use Trial Closes Throughout A Presentation (Step 1: On every single slide, embed a yes-or-no question where the answer is obviously yes; Step 2: After EVERY testimonial, future pace with trial closes - "Can you imagine what that would be like?", "Isn't that amazing?", "Are you getting excited?"; Step 3: When showing features, ask "How many of you guys want this?"; Step 4: Use the phrases: "Does this make sense?", "You guys getting this?", "Right?", "Awesome?"; Step 5: Target a minimum of 1,000 yes responses across the full presentation)
- MET-RB-009: How To Structure The Stack Slide Sequence (Step 1: Present Component 1, then show Stack Slide with Component 1 and its value; Step 2: Present Component 2, then show Stack Slide with Component 1 + Component 2 and cumulative value; Step 3: Continue stacking for each component; Step 4: Show full stack with total value; Step 5: Use 3 if-all statements to get agreement the total value is justified; Step 6: Reveal actual discounted price; Step 7: Drop additional closes; Step 8: Restack one final time; Step 9: Show final slide with offer recap, green price, URL/go, countdown clock, and pre-loaded Q&A)
- MET-RB-010: How To Monetize The Thank You Page To Cover Ad Costs (Step 1: Create thank you page video re-exciting them about the webinar; Step 2: Display date/time prominently with write-down reminder; Step 3: Add re-registration backup link; Step 4: Offer a free trial of your software or a low-cost product ($27 book/guide); Step 5: Add a second offer if possible - e.g., Step 1 = write down time, Step 2 = get free trial, Step 3 = buy my book; Step 6: Goal is to recoup $3-5 per registration ad cost before the webinar even happens)
