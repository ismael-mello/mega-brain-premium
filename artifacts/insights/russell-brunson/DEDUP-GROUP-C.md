## MENTAL MODELS (MM)

- ID: MM-RB-200
  - Name: The Vehicle Model
  - Description: Customers are in a current vehicle trying to reach a result on a mountain. Your job is to get them OUT of their current vehicle and INTO your new vehicle, not to make their current vehicle go faster.
  - Application: Use when crafting any offer positioning -- always frame as a new opportunity (switch), not improvement of their current approach.
  - Module_Sources: M06

- ID: MM-RB-201
  - Name: The Big Domino
  - Description: In any sales argument, there is ONE thing -- if they believe this one thing, they have to give you money. The entire presentation exists to knock over this single domino. Every webinar must focus on ONE thing you are trying to get someone to believe.
  - Application: Use when designing any presentation or sales letter -- identify the single belief that, if accepted, makes the purchase inevitable.
  - Module_Sources: M06, M07

- ID: MM-RB-202
  - Name: The Helicopter Rescue Model
  - Description: Like rescue helicopters saving drowning people, you can only save the people swimming toward you. Serve the most predisposed buyers first, and others will follow.
  - Application: Use when prioritizing which audience segments to target -- serve eager buyers first rather than trying to convince skeptics.
  - Module_Sources: M06

- ID: MM-RB-203
  - Name: The Four-Minute Mile Effect
  - Description: Once one person breaks a barrier (like John Reese making $1M in a day), it becomes possible for everyone. Create this proof point inside your community.
  - Application: Use when building culture and community -- create an aspirational achievement that, once broken by one member, unlocks belief for all.
  - Module_Sources: M06

- ID: MM-RB-204
  - Name: Switch vs Stack
  - Description: A Switch webinar moves people from an old vehicle to a new one (done ONCE per business). A Stack webinar adds value within the vehicle they are already in. Every business needs both but starts with the switch.
  - Application: Use when planning product launches and ongoing offers -- one switch to acquire, then stack to monetize.
  - Module_Sources: M06

- ID: MM-RB-205
  - Name: The Three Core Markets
  - Description: All businesses fall into one of three money markets: Health, Wealth, or Relationships. You must pick one and go laser-focused within it.
  - Application: Use when defining your market position -- identify which of the three core markets you serve, then niche down.
  - Module_Sources: M06

- ID: MM-RB-206
  - Name: Red Ocean to Blue Ocean via Niche
  - Description: Take one step back from the reddest Red Ocean sub-market, change 2-3 things, and create a new offer. This is how you create a Blue Ocean from a proven market.
  - Application: Use when entering a competitive market -- don't avoid competition, take a step back and differentiate 2-3 elements.
  - Module_Sources: M06

- ID: MM-RB-207
  - Name: Memory Association Pricing
  - Description: The human brain associates the price with the LAST thing it saw. The Stack ensures the price is associated with the ENTIRE offer, not just the last small bonus.
  - Application: Use when designing offer presentations -- always re-stack all components before revealing price so the brain links the price to the total package.
  - Module_Sources: M06

- ID: MM-RB-208
  - Name: The ER Penalty
  - Description: Any word ending in "ER" -- better, faster, easier -- signals improvement, not new opportunity. Improvement offers are inherently harder to sell.
  - Application: Use as a copywriting filter -- scan your messaging for ER words and replace with new opportunity language.
  - Module_Sources: M06

- ID: MM-RB-209
  - Name: Identity Protection Moat
  - Description: When customers self-identify with your brand, competitors become irrelevant. Leaving you means leaving their entire identity behind, which is psychologically very hard.
  - Application: Use when building long-term retention strategy -- create identity markers (t-shirts, awards, titles) that make leaving psychologically costly.
  - Module_Sources: M06

- ID: MM-RB-210
  - Name: The Title of Liberty
  - Description: Create a rallying standard that people can look toward -- a symbol or manifesto that inspires people to follow you into battle against the status quo.
  - Application: Use when building your movement's visual and emotional anchors -- a flag, a manifesto, a battle cry.
  - Module_Sources: M06

- ID: MM-RB-211
  - Name: The Three False Belief Patterns
  - Description: Every prospect has three types of false beliefs: about the Vehicle/opportunity itself, Internal beliefs about their own ability, and External beliefs about circumstances. Smash each one and rebuild it with a new empowering belief; when all three are destroyed, the prospect has no legs to stand on.
  - Application: Use when structuring any persuasive presentation -- identify and address all three false belief categories systematically.
  - Module_Sources: M06, M07

- ID: MM-RB-212
  - Name: Teacher Mode Kills Sales
  - Description: The fastest way to destroy your webinar conversion is to switch into teacher mode with step-by-step instruction. Teach through stories and belief pattern replacement instead.

- ID: MM-RB-213
  - Name: Blind Offers Create Desire
