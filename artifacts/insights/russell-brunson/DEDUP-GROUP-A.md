## L2: MENTAL MODELS (MM) - 24 Unique Elements

---

### MM-RB-001
- **Name:** The Two Books Model
- **Description:** DotCom Secrets (DCS) is the strategy/mechanics of funnels and traffic. Expert Secrets (ES) is the positioning/messaging/movement-building. They work together - DCS builds the machine, ES fills it with believers.
- **Application:** Use to understand which problem you're solving: "Is this a funnel/traffic problem (DCS) or a messaging/positioning problem (ES)?"
- **Module_Sources:** M00

---

### MM-RB-002
- **Name:** Doodle-to-Understanding
- **Description:** If you can't draw a concept on a napkin/whiteboard, you don't truly understand it. Russell draws everything - funnels, value ladders, traffic flows. The doodle IS the understanding.
- **Application:** Test your understanding of any business concept by trying to draw it. If you can't sketch it, study more.
- **Module_Sources:** M00

---

### MM-RB-003
- **Name:** Profitability Arms Race
- **Description:** Constant innovation is required because competition copies everything. As soon as your funnel works, competitors will model it. The only defense is to keep innovating on the offer, the hooks, and the experience faster than competitors can copy.
- **Application:** When competition catches up and margins compress, innovate on the offer rather than cutting prices.
- **Module_Sources:** M00

---

### MM-RB-004
- **Name:** Expert + Business = Exponential Growth
- **Description:** Expert personality as growth accelerant. A business without an expert is linear growth. Add an expert personality and growth becomes exponential because movements form around people, not products.
- **Application:** Any business can add an expert personality layer to accelerate growth beyond what marketing alone can achieve.
- **Module_Sources:** M00

---

### MM-RB-005
- **Name:** Cult-Building Model
- **Description:** Tribe loyalty is more valuable than direct response copywriting skill. When you build a tribe/cult following, you can become mediocre at selling and still make money because your people buy based on identity and belonging, not persuasion.
- **Application:** Invest in community, identity markers, shared language, and shared enemies - not just better copy.
- **Module_Sources:** M00, M01A

---

### MM-RB-006
- **Name:** Red Ocean / Blue Ocean Market Depth (3 Levels)
- **Description:** Markets have 3 levels of depth: 1) Core Markets (health, wealth, relationships) - red ocean; 2) Sub-Markets (weight loss, real estate investing, dating) - still competitive; 3) Your Created Niche (ketogenic diet for busy moms, Airbnb investing for millennials) - blue ocean. Create your niche at Level 3.
- **Application:** Never compete at Level 1 or 2. Go to Level 3 by combining elements in a unique way to create a niche where you have no direct competition.
- **Module_Sources:** M01A

---

### MM-RB-007
- **Name:** Prolific Index
- **Description:** On a spectrum from Boring to Crazy, most successful movements sit in the middle-right: prolific but not insane. Too boring = no following. Too crazy = no credibility. The sweet spot is being bold enough to attract attention but grounded enough to maintain trust.
- **Application:** Audit your positioning on the Prolific Index. Push toward bolder if you're being ignored. Pull back if you're losing credibility.
- **Module_Sources:** M01A

---

### MM-RB-008
- **Name:** Ecosystem Model
- **Description:** Competitors become partners when you create your own blue ocean niche. If you're in the same sub-market but different niches, you serve different segments and can cross-promote. Competition disappears and collaboration emerges.
- **Application:** Identify people in your sub-market but different niches and build partnerships instead of treating them as threats.
- **Module_Sources:** M01A

---

### MM-RB-009
- **Name:** Polarity Model
- **Description:** Neutrality kills influence and income. You must create polar zones - strong positions that attract passionate supporters AND passionate detractors. The money is in the polarity, not the middle. People with no opinion have no followers.
- **Application:** Take strong, clear positions on controversial topics in your niche. Don't try to appeal to everyone.
- **Module_Sources:** M01A

---

### MM-RB-010
- **Name:** Four-Minute Mile Belief Model
- **Description:** Once Roger Bannister broke the 4-minute mile, dozens followed within months. People need to SEE someone do it before they believe they can. Your job as a leader is to be the Bannister moment for your followers - or create one.
- **Application:** Create "proof of concept" stories - your own or your students' - that shatter limiting beliefs about what's possible.
- **Module_Sources:** M01A

---

### MM-RB-011
- **Name:** Pattern Recognition as Core Skill
- **Description:** The most valuable skill in business is not any tactic but the ability to recognize patterns across businesses, markets, and funnels. Russell's success comes from seeing patterns others miss and applying them in new contexts.
- **Application:** Study multiple businesses, funnels, and offers. Look for the pattern underneath the surface tactics.
- **Module_Sources:** M01A

---

### MM-RB-012
- **Name:** Vehicle Metaphor (New Opportunity)
- **Description:** Selling improvement = asking someone to fix their broken-down car. Selling new opportunity = offering them a brand-new car. People emotionally prefer a new vehicle over repairing the old one, even if repair is logically cheaper. The emotion of "new" beats the logic of "better."
- **Application:** Frame your offer as a new vehicle, not an upgrade to their existing vehicle. Never use "-ER" words (better, faster, easier).
- **Module_Sources:** M01B

---

### MM-RB-013
- **Name:** Status as Universal Filter
- **Description:** Every purchase decision runs through a status filter: will this increase my status, decrease it, or keep it neutral? Spending money = status decrease (losing resources). Guaranteed results = status neutral. Transformation = status increase. Your offer must demonstrate clear status increase to sell.
- **Application:** For every element of your offer, ask: "Does this increase my customer's perceived status?" If not, reframe it.
- **Module_Sources:** M01B

---

### MM-RB-014
- **Name:** Status Seesaw
- **Description:** When you gain status in one area, you often lose it in another. Someone who gets rich may lose status with old friends. Someone who gets fit may intimidate their partner. You must address BOTH sides of the seesaw in your marketing.
- **Application:** Address both the desired status gain AND the feared status loss in your messaging. "You'll succeed AND your friends will still respect you."
- **Module_Sources:** M01B

---

### MM-RB-015
- **Name:** Sophistication Levels by Market
- **Description:** Markets have different levels of sophistication. A new market accepts simple claims. A sophisticated market has heard every promise. Your messaging must match the sophistication level of your market - what works in a naive market will fail in a sophisticated one.
- **Application:** Assess your market's sophistication level before crafting messaging. More sophisticated markets need more nuance, proof, and specificity.
- **Module_Sources:** M01B

---

### MM-RB-016
- **Name:** Opportunity Switch Level Model
- **Description:** An opportunity switch can happen at the niche level (smaller pivot, keep the sub-market) or at the sub-market level (bigger pivot, completely new arena). The bigger the switch, the more dramatic the emotional impact, but the harder the transition for the customer.
- **Application:** Choose your switch level strategically. Niche-level switches are easier to sell but less dramatic. Sub-market switches are harder but more transformative.
- **Module_Sources:** M01B

---

### MM-RB-017
- **Name:** Front End vs Back End Offer Psychology
- **Description:** Front-end offers attract prospects - they should be irresistible, low-risk, and designed to build trust. Back-end offers monetize the relationship - they can be high-ticket because trust already exists. The psychology of buying is fundamentally different at each stage.
- **Application:** Never try to make money on the front end. Use front end for trust-building, back end for profit.
- **Module_Sources:** M01B

---

### MM-RB-018
- **Name:** The Secret Formula (Who, Where, Bait, Result)
- **Description:** Four questions that define every business: 1) Who is your dream customer? 2) Where are they congregating? 3) What bait will attract them? 4) What result/destination will you take them to? Everything in the DotCom Secrets system flows from answering these.
- **Application:** Before building ANY funnel, answer all 4 questions. If you can't answer them clearly, you're not ready to build.
- **Module_Sources:** M02

---

### MM-RB-019
- **Name:** The Congregation Principle
- **Description:** People congregate based on beliefs, values, and interests - just like churches. The internet enables congregations around every niche imaginable. Your job is to find where your target market congregates and throw your bait into that pond. Don't fish in the ocean - find the pond.
- **Application:** Research where your dream customers already spend time: forums, Facebook groups, YouTube channels, podcasts, email lists. Target those congregations.
- **Module_Sources:** M02

---

### MM-RB-020
- **Name:** Three Types of Traffic
- **Description:** All traffic falls into 3 categories: 1) Traffic You Control - paid ads you can direct to any page; 2) Traffic You Don't Control - organic, SEO, social shares; 3) Traffic You Own - your email list. The goal of types 1 and 2 is ALWAYS to convert to type 3. Traffic you own is the ultimate asset.
- **Application:** Every marketing activity should have ONE goal: get the person onto your email list. Paid ads go to squeeze pages. Blog posts have opt-ins. Social media drives to landing pages.
- **Module_Sources:** M02

---

### MM-RB-021
- **Name:** The Value Ladder Mental Model
- **Description:** Customers enter at the bottom with low-cost items, receive value, and naturally want to ascend to higher levels. Each level provides more value at a higher price. Like sushi: you eat it, love it, want more, want classes, want VIP experiences. Each funnel is also a mini value ladder in itself.
- **Application:** Design your business as a ladder. Free -> low ticket -> medium ticket -> high ticket -> continuity. Each rung delivers enough value to create desire for the next.
- **Module_Sources:** M02

---

### MM-RB-022
- **Name:** The Self-Liquidating Offer (SLO)
- **Description:** Your initial funnel's goal is to break even - acquiring customers at zero net cost. If your front-end funnel breaks even (revenue = ad spend), you acquire customers for free. Then ALL back-end revenue is pure profit. This is how you outspend every competitor.
- **Application:** Design your front-end funnel to break even, not to profit. Measure success by cost per acquired customer, not by front-end revenue.
- **Module_Sources:** M02

---

### MM-RB-023
- **Name:** The Reverse Engineering Mindset
- **Description:** Before building anything, reverse engineer what is already working. Buy competitors' products. Transcribe their sales videos. Map their upsells, downsells, price points, traffic sources, ad copy, and landing pages. If an ugly page is ranked #1 on ClickBank, don't try to make it prettier - model it.
- **Application:** Before creating any funnel, spend days or weeks reverse engineering 3-5 successful competitors in your market. Model what works.
- **Module_Sources:** M02

---

### MM-RB-024
- **Name:** The Raise Model
- **Description:** "How can I get myself a raise today?" Each new traffic source you add is a permanent raise. Find a site that gets you 100 profitable leads per day, lock it in, find another. Your income compounds with each new source, and each source continues to produce without additional work.
- **Application:** Treat each new traffic source as a pay raise. Add one at a time, validate profitability, then stack the next one.
- **Module_Sources:** M02
