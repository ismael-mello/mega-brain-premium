## L1: PHILOSOPHIES (14)

- PHI-RB-001: One Thing Belief Principle (If your sales message tries to convince people of more than one thing, it bombs. The only consistency in blockbuster wins is getting them to believe ONE thing through the sales message. Learned from Perry Belcher.)

- PHI-RB-002: The Big Domino Philosophy (Instead of trying to knock down a thousand different objections, find the ONE big domino that if knocked down makes all other concerns, objections, and everything else irrelevant or disappear. Inspired by Tim Ferriss's daily thinking practice of finding the one domino that knocks all others down.)

- PHI-RB-003: Story Over Copywriting (Good copy is just good story. If you understand good story structure, persuasion becomes easy. You do not need to be a gifted writer -- you need to be able to tell stories and connect emotionally.)

- PHI-RB-004: Emotional Over Logical Selling (People buy based on emotion, not logic. The epiphany bridge connects people emotionally rather than logically. Logic does not create belief -- emotional experiences do.)

- PHI-RB-005: Belief Is The Only Currency (Sales is not about convincing people of features or benefits. Sales is about creating belief. If you create enough belief that your thing is the key and only attainable through your vehicle, they HAVE to give you money or they are lying to themselves.)

- PHI-RB-006: Conflict Creates Emotion, Not Desire (In stories, people think emotion comes from the desire, but nobody cares about what the hero is trying to get. What causes the emotion is the CONFLICT in the middle. Without conflict, there is no good story.)

- PHI-RB-007: Transformation Over Achievement (The journey of achievement is what the hero sets out to do, but what the audience actually cares about is the journey of transformation -- who the hero BECOMES. Lightning McQueen stopping before the finish line to help the King matters more than winning the Piston Cup.)

- PHI-RB-008: Vulnerability Creates Connection (When you share your deep internal desires -- the ones nobody talks about -- it creates instant rapport. People are never willing to share at that level, so when you do it first, they connect immediately.)

- PHI-RB-009: You Are Your Customer Five Years Ago (If you cannot figure out the false beliefs your customers have, think about what YOUR false beliefs were before you had your epiphanies. You were your audience before you found the new opportunity.)

- PHI-RB-010: Investment Creates Commitment (People do not value what they get for free. The Coach Schultz story: "If I give this tape for free, you would put it on the shelf. You never would have watched it. But now you've invested in it, so now you're going to watch it and become better because of that.")

- PHI-RB-011: Every Expert Has The Same Opportunity (Everyone is "one funnel away." Each person in the audience represents lives they can change. The presentation is about helping them see that one funnel can bridge the gap between where they are and where they want to be.)

- PHI-RB-012: Practice Makes Story Perfect (The biggest key to getting good at telling stories is telling a LOT of stories. Russell has told his potato gun story "billions of times." He tells stories on his daily podcast, to his office team, to his wife, to his kids -- 5 to 8 times before using them live.)

- PHI-RB-013: Curiosity Is The Lifeblood Of Sales (90% of this business is curiosity, getting people to think "what is that thing?" If the audience can figure out the answer just by reading the headline, they will not show up. Open loops that cannot be closed without attending.)

- PHI-RB-014: Chain Breaking Philosophy (Dysfunction, limiting beliefs, and negative patterns are generational chains. The person who breaks the chain -- the "chain breaker" -- transforms not just themselves but their entire lineage. This is the deepest form of the transformation journey.)

## L2: MENTAL MODELS (12)

- MM-RB-001: The Big Domino Model (Every sales message has ONE belief that, if established, causes all objections and concerns to become irrelevant and forces the prospect to buy. Structure: "If I can make them believe that [new opportunity] is the key to [what they desire most] and is only attainable through [my specific vehicle], then all objections become irrelevant and they have to give me money.")

- MM-RB-002: Blue Ocean vs Red Ocean Selling (If your Big Domino statement is not a logically true statement -- if the customer can get the same result from 18,000 other products -- you are in a red ocean. The Big Domino must place you in a blue ocean where your vehicle is the ONLY way to get the result.)

- MM-RB-003: Three False Belief Categories (The three types of false beliefs that prop up the Big Domino and prevent it from falling: 1) False beliefs about the VEHICLE -- "I don't believe funnels work"; 2) False beliefs about THEMSELVES (internal) -- "I'm not techie enough"; 3) False beliefs about EXTERNAL things -- "I don't know how to drive traffic.")

- MM-RB-004: Experience -> Story -> Belief Chain (Every belief a person holds was created through a specific chain: they had an EXPERIENCE, from that experience they created a STORY they tell themselves, and that story became a BELIEF pattern. To change the belief, you must replace their story with a better one using an Epiphany Bridge story.)

- MM-RB-005: Expert Layer vs Product Layer (When selling a commodity product, making the Big Domino statement is nearly impossible because customers can buy the same thing elsewhere. Moving one layer back to the EXPERT level "uncommoditizes" the offering and makes the Big Domino attainable.)

- MM-RB-006: Mountain and Valley Positioning (As an expert, people see you up on the mountain of achievement. To create empathy and connection, you must come back DOWN to where the audience is: "I was in this exact same spot. I remember what it was like and it sucked. There's some cool stuff over here I can show you.")

- MM-RB-007: The Zeigarnik Effect in Sales (A waiter remembers an order perfectly until it is completed, then forgets it immediately. Until a cognitive loop is closed, the brain cannot let go of it. Use this by opening curiosity loops in headlines, emails, and teasers that can only be closed by attending the presentation.)

- MM-RB-008: Price Marinating (Myron Golden's concept: introduce the price early and leave it visible throughout the entire presentation. First reaction is shock, but from that point forward, the rest of the presentation proves the value. By the time you close, the price has been "marinating" for the full 90 minutes.)

- MM-RB-009: Story Stacking (Telling multiple stories about the same objection or belief, one after another, each knocking it down from a different angle. Like dominoes tipping -- first they think "maybe" then "okay" then "I have no more excuses." Russell does this naturally when handling the "I can't afford it" objection with the Rich Dad Poor Dad story, then the raking leaves story, then the buddy financing story.)

- MM-RB-010: Audience Maturity Levels (The same offer may be a huge new opportunity for one audience and old news for another. Network marketers hearing about online business -- that is a massive new opportunity for them. Internet marketers hearing about online business -- that is not new at all. The Big Domino must be calibrated to the audience's maturity level.)

- MM-RB-011: One-to-One vs One-to-Many Selling (In one-on-one selling, you can tailor the story to the specific person's situation. In one-to-many, you must make the message inclusive so everyone feels it speaks to them. At the beginning, address different segments: "How many of you are just getting started? How many have a successful business?" -- so everyone feels it is for them.)

- MM-RB-012: Story Inventory Model (Dan Kennedy's concept: build a lifetime inventory of stories mapped to every objection you ever receive. Write them down, practice them, so you can pull them out instantly when needed. The more you practice telling them, the faster they pop out of your mind when you need them in the moment.)

## L3: HEURISTICS (16)

- HEUR-RB-001: One Belief Per Sales Message (If your sales message tries to convince them of MORE than one thing, it will bomb. One belief = one message. Every blockbuster win has this as the common thread.)

- HEUR-RB-002: Big Domino Validity Test (Read your Big Domino statement. If it is not a TRUE logical statement, it means: 1) You are not in the prolific zone, 2) You are not offering a new opportunity, 3) You are not in a blue ocean. Go back and fix your positioning.)

- HEUR-RB-003: First 10% Rapport Rule (In every movie, the first 10% of the story is dedicated entirely to building rapport with the hero. In screenplays, this is approximately page 22 when the hero physically leaves their safe location. Apply this: spend the first 10% of your presentation building rapport before asking for anything.)

- HEUR-RB-004: Two of Five Rapport Elements (To build rapport with the audience, a character needs at least 2 of these 5 qualities: 1) Victim, 2) Something in jeopardy, 3) Likable, 4) Funny, 5) Powerful. If you do not have at least two, the audience will not care about your story.)

- HEUR-RB-005: Five to Eight Story Rehearsals Before Live (Tell your story 5 to 8 times to different people -- your team, your spouse, your kids -- before using it in a real sales situation. This lets you feel where people connect and where they disconnect, so you can refine it.)

- HEUR-RB-006: Minimum 10 False Beliefs Inventory (Write out at least 10 false beliefs your customers have, along with: the experience that created each belief, the story they tell themselves because of it, and the Epiphany Bridge story you have to replace it. Long term, build far more than 10.)

- HEUR-RB-007: The 5-7 Why's Rule For Internal Desire (To uncover someone's true internal desire, keep asking "why" -- usually 5 to 7 times -- until they break down and become vulnerable. The surface answer is never the real answer. The real desire is always buried deeper.)

- HEUR-RB-008: Webinar Video Registration Kills Conversions (Russell has never tested a video on a webinar registration page that increased conversions. Videos always destroy conversion on registration pages. Use curiosity-based headlines and a button only. No video.)

- HEUR-RB-009: Call It A Web Class Not A Webinar (The word "webinar" is a super nerdy techno-babble term that regular people do not understand. When going to cold markets, call it a "web class" -- conversion is much better.)

- HEUR-RB-010: One Day Minimum For Headlines (Figuring out the One Thing and the Three Secrets headlines typically takes at least a full day in front of a whiteboard. Do not stress if it does not come in 10-15 minutes. It sometimes takes an entire day of iteration.)

- HEUR-RB-011: 98% of Hollywood Films Follow Hero's Two Journeys (Michael Hague's research shows that 98% of all Hollywood films fit the Hero's Two Journeys structure. On specific pages of the screenplay, specific things always happen. This universal pattern can be applied to sales storytelling.)

- HEUR-RB-012: Transcribe And Study Great Pitches (Send any great sales video or webinar to Rev.com for transcription. Read through 10-15 times to reverse-engineer how they structure stories, hooks, and closes. Jason Fladlien is Russell's example of the best webinar pitcher to study.)

- HEUR-RB-013: Physical Leaving In Video (In storytelling and video, physically LEAVE a location to bring the audience on a journey. Frodo leaves the Shire, Lightning McQueen leaves the racetrack. In your videos, do not just stand at a desk -- get in your car, walk somewhere, bring people along.)

- HEUR-RB-014: Headline Cannot Be Answerable (If the audience can figure out the answer to your headline just by reading it, they will not attend. Test: read the headline and ask "can I figure this out without attending?" If yes, rewrite. "How to ethically steal over a million dollars of funnel hacks from your competitors for under $100" -- you cannot figure that out without attending.)

- HEUR-RB-015: Three Pre-Seduction Videos Before Webinar (Between registration and the live webinar, send 3 teaser videos -- one per day -- each teasing one of the Three Secrets. Do NOT give the answer. Tease it, build excitement, and tell them they must be on the webinar to get the full answer.)

- HEUR-RB-016: Crappy Video Beats Empty Placeholder (A crappy video in a funnel is better than an empty placeholder. If you have a crappy video, you will be motivated to make a better one later. If you leave it empty, you will never finish and never launch.)

## L4: FRAMEWORKS (16)

- FW-RB-001: Big Domino Statement Framework (Template: "If I can make them believe that [my new opportunity] is the key to [what they desire most] and is only attainable through [my specific vehicle], then all of the objections and concerns become irrelevant and they have to give me money." Three components: New Opportunity, Desire, Specific Vehicle.)

- FW-RB-002: Three False Beliefs Framework (Every prospect's Big Domino is propped up by three categories of false beliefs: 1) Vehicle beliefs -- "This approach doesn't work"; 2) Internal beliefs -- "I can't do it personally"; 3) External beliefs -- "Something outside my control prevents me." Identify the #1 core false belief in each category.)

- FW-RB-003: Hero's Two Journeys Framework (Michael Hague's Hollywood storytelling framework. Every great story has two parallel journeys: Journey #1 - Achievement (the external goal the hero is pursuing); Journey #2 - Transformation (the internal change the hero undergoes). The audience cares more about the transformation than whether the hero achieves the external goal.)

- FW-RB-004: Plot Statement Framework (Three core elements of any story: 1) CHARACTER -- who is the hero; 2) DESIRE -- what do they want; 3) CONFLICT -- what stands in the way. Template: "[Character] had a desire to [desire], but on the journey there was conflict: [conflict].")

- FW-RB-005: Five Rapport Building Elements (From Michael Hague -- to make an audience care about a character, include at least 2 of these 5: 1) Victim -- someone bad things happen to; 2) Jeopardy -- something important is at stake; 3) Likable -- genuinely pleasant or relatable; 4) Funny -- humor creates connection; 5) Powerful -- competence and capability.)

- FW-RB-006: Four Types of Desire (The hero's desire in any story is typically one of four things: 1) WIN something -- a race, a competition, a person; 2) RETRIEVE something -- go somewhere and bring something back; 3) ESCAPE something -- get away from danger or a bad situation; 4) STOP something -- prevent something bad from happening.)

- FW-RB-007: External vs Internal Desires Framework (Every person has two levels of desire: EXTERNAL -- the surface-level desire they are willing to share publicly ("I want to make money", "I want to lose weight"); INTERNAL -- the deep, vulnerable desire they rarely share ("I want my dad to be proud of me", "I want my wife to respect me"). The external desire maps to the Journey of Achievement; the internal desire maps to the Journey of Transformation.)

- FW-RB-008: Epiphany Bridge Script (8-Element Story Framework) (The complete storytelling framework with 8 elements: 1) BACKSTORY -- what gives the audience a vested interest in your journey; 2) DESIRES -- external struggles and internal desires; 3) THE WALL -- the problem within your current opportunity that blocked you; 4) THE EPIPHANY -- the aha moment that introduced you to the new opportunity; 5) THE PLAN -- what you created to achieve your desire in the new vehicle; 6) THE CONFLICT -- what went wrong along the way; 7) THE ACHIEVEMENT -- the external result you got; 8) THE TRANSFORMATION -- how you changed as a person internally.)

- FW-RB-009: Kinda Like Bridge Framework (Whenever you hit technical jargon or a complex concept in your story, stop and say "it's kinda like [something they already understand]." Bridge the unknown to the known instantly. Example: ketones = "It's kinda like a million motivational speakers running through your body giving you insane amounts of energy.")

- FW-RB-010: False Belief Replacement Framework (Four-column framework for breaking false beliefs: Column 1) False Chain of Belief -- what they believe; Column 2) The Experience -- what happened that gave them that belief; Column 3) The Story -- the narrative running in their head; Column 4) New Epiphany Bridge -- your replacement story that trumps theirs and creates a new belief.)

- FW-RB-011: Three Secrets Framework (The webinar structure for knocking down the Big Domino: Secret #1 addresses the vehicle false belief; Secret #2 addresses the internal false belief; Secret #3 addresses the external false belief. Each secret is a curiosity-based headline wrapping an Epiphany Bridge story designed to destroy that specific false belief category.)

- FW-RB-012: Origin Story -> Three Secrets -> Close Sequence (Full presentation flow: 1) Introduce the Big Domino statement; 2) Tell origin story as first attempt to knock the domino down; 3) If domino did not fall, address vehicle beliefs with Secret #1 sub-stories; 4) Address internal beliefs with Secret #2 sub-stories; 5) Address external beliefs with Secret #3 sub-stories; 6) By end, Big Domino must have fallen; 7) Move into close and pitch.)

- FW-RB-013: Wufoo Form Story Collection Framework (Kaelin Poulin's method for scaling storytelling: 1) Create a form with the 8 Epiphany Bridge questions; 2) Send to customers/community members who have had results; 3) They write out their full story in the form; 4) Read all submissions, identify the 2-3 most emotionally powerful; 5) Bring those people on Facebook Live and interview them, guiding them through their story; 6) Use those videos as testimonials that convert the exact target market.)

- FW-RB-014: Pre-Seduction Sequence Framework (Three-video indoctrination sequence between webinar registration and the live event: Day 1 email/video teases Secret #1; Day 2 email/video teases Secret #2; Day 3 email/video teases Secret #3. Each video builds curiosity without revealing the answer. After registration, many people put the origin story video on the thank-you page.)

- FW-RB-015: The Wall and New Opportunity Model (In every origin story, the hero hits a WALL within their current opportunity -- the old vehicle that physically cannot get them where they want to go. The wall forces them to leave the old vehicle and discover the new opportunity. The new vehicle is the epiphany. "I was stuck in this vehicle. I hit this wall. I cannot get forward because the vehicle sucks.")

- FW-RB-016: Inclusive Opening Framework (For one-to-many selling, open by addressing multiple audience segments so everyone feels included: "How many of you are just getting started? Cool, this is perfect for you. How many of you have a successful business already? Awesome, this is for you too." This prevents people from self-selecting out during the presentation.)

## L5: METHODOLOGIES (10)

- MET-RB-001: Big Domino Creation Methodology (Step 1: Write the Big Domino statement using the template. Step 2: Read it as a logical statement -- is it TRUE? Step 3: If not true, check: Are you in the prolific zone? Are you offering a new opportunity? Are you in a blue ocean? Step 4: Iterate and massage until the statement is logically airtight -- meaning if they believe it, they MUST buy from you and nobody else. Step 5: This may take multiple iterations and days of thinking.)

- MET-RB-002: False Belief Inventory Methodology (Step 1: Write at least 10 false beliefs your customers have that prevent them from buying. Step 2: For each, identify what EXPERIENCE gave them that belief. Step 3: For each, write the STORY they tell themselves. Step 4: For each, identify or create an Epiphany Bridge story that replaces their story. Step 5: Categorize each into Vehicle / Internal / External. Step 6: Circle the #1 biggest false belief in each category. Step 7: These become your Three Secrets.)

- MET-RB-003: Epiphany Bridge Story Writing Process (Step 1: Answer 8 questions -- What is your backstory? What are your external desires? Internal desires? What wall did you hit? What was your epiphany? What plan did you create? What conflict did you face? What did you achieve and how did you transform? Step 2: Write the story answering all 8 questions. Step 3: Tell it live -- Facebook Live or video recording. Step 4: Watch yourself, critique, identify where you lose people. Step 5: Retell it, refine, repeat 5-8 times until it flows naturally.)

- MET-RB-004: Origin Story Facebook Live Method (Step 1: Write out the 8-element Epiphany Bridge script. Step 2: Open Facebook on your phone. Step 3: Click "Live" (or just "Create Video" if nervous). Step 4: Tell your origin story using the outline. Step 5: Do not stress about perfection -- first time will be rough. Step 6: Watch the recording and self-critique. Step 7: Retell it to friends, family, team. Step 8: Repeat until you feel the audience connection points and can tell it fluidly.)

- MET-RB-005: Customer Story Collection Method (Kaelin Poulin's method. Step 1: Create a Wufoo form or similar with the 8 Epiphany Bridge questions. Step 2: Send to community members who have achieved results. Step 3: Have them write out their full story. Step 4: Read all submissions, select the 2-3 most emotionally powerful. Step 5: Get selected people on Facebook Live. Step 6: Interview them, prompting them to tell specific details when they gloss over emotional moments. Step 7: These videos become powerful testimonials for the exact target market you want to attract.)

- MET-RB-006: Three Secrets Headline Creation Process (Step 1: Take each of the 3 core false beliefs (vehicle, internal, external). Step 2: Know the Epiphany Bridge story associated with each. Step 3: Create a curiosity-based headline that makes it IMPOSSIBLE to figure out the answer without attending. Step 4: Test: Can someone read this headline and know the answer? If yes, rewrite. Step 5: Iterate -- this may take an entire day in front of a whiteboard. Step 6: Use formats like "How to [result] without [expected method]" or unique/exotic framing.)

- MET-RB-007: Pre-Seduction Video Creation Method (Step 1: After registration page is created, record 3 short horizontal videos. Step 2: Video 1 teases Secret #1 -- share just enough to create massive curiosity, do NOT reveal the answer. Step 3: Video 2 teases Secret #2 -- same approach. Step 4: Video 3 teases Secret #3 -- same approach. Step 5: Upload to YouTube as unlisted. Step 6: Embed in ClickFunnels pages. Step 7: Send one per day via email leading up to the webinar.)

- MET-RB-008: Pitch Reverse Engineering Method (Step 1: Find the best salespeople and presenters in your space -- Jason Fladlien, Tony Robbins events, etc. Step 2: Record or obtain their presentations. Step 3: Send to Rev.com for transcription. Step 4: Read the transcription 10-15 times. Step 5: Identify their story hooks, bridges, closes, and transitions. Step 6: Practice telling those stories or using those patterns until they become natural speech patterns. Step 7: Adapt them to your own presentations.)

- MET-RB-009: Story Selling Objection Handling Method (Step 1: Every time you receive a concern, objection, or refund reason -- record it. Step 2: For each objection, find or develop an Epiphany Bridge story that neutralizes it. Step 3: Practice telling each story until it is second nature. Step 4: Weave these stories throughout your presentations to preemptively address objections. Step 5: Over time, run your webinar live multiple times, watching chat for new objections, and add stories to handle each one until the Big Domino falls completely.)

- MET-RB-010: Morgan's Origin Story Coaching Demo (Russell demonstrates live coaching of a student's origin story. Step 1: Have the student tell their raw story. Step 2: Get group feedback on where connection was strong vs. where it faded. Step 3: Identify: Was the epiphany bridge clear? Was vulnerability present? Were specific details used instead of glossing over? Step 4: Retell the story yourself as the coach, modeling better emotional engagement, painting the environment, and making the audience a participant in the story. Step 5: Student refines and retells.)
