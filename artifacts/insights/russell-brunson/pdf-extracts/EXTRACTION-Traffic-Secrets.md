## L2: MENTAL MODELS (MM)

### MM-RB-PDF-001
- **statement:** "3 Core Markets / 3 Core Desires: Health, Wealth, Relationships. Every business exists within one of these three."
- **context:** All human desires funnel into three markets. Your submarket sits within one of these. Understanding which core desire you serve determines your messaging.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 30-31

### MM-RB-PDF-002
- **statement:** "Searcher vs Scroller: Two types of online behavior that require completely different strategies."
- **context:** Searchers actively look for solutions (Google, YouTube search). Scrollers are passively browsing (Facebook, Instagram feeds). Different hooks, different funnels.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 42-44

### MM-RB-PDF-003
- **statement:** "3 Types of Traffic: Traffic you earn (organic), Traffic you control (paid), Traffic you own (your list)."
- **context:** The most critical traffic framework. All earned and controlled traffic must be converted into owned traffic. Your list is the only traffic you truly control.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 56-58

### MM-RB-PDF-004
- **statement:** "Platform Lifecycle: Adoption (free reach) → Price Hike (pay to play) → Slap/Snap (algorithm change destroys businesses)."
- **context:** Every platform follows this pattern—Google, Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat. Understanding this cycle lets you get in early and prepare for the inevitable slap.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 4-6, 204

### MM-RB-PDF-005
- **statement:** "Away from Pain vs Toward Pleasure: Two fundamental motivators that drive all customer behavior."
- **context:** Some customers are motivated to escape pain, others to pursue pleasure. Your messaging must identify which motivator applies to your dream customer.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 38-39

### MM-RB-PDF-006
- **statement:** "The Shadow Funnel: The invisible journey people take researching you after seeing your ad but before buying."
- **context:** When someone sees your ad and doesn't buy immediately, they search your name, company, and product in Google. This "shadow funnel" creates traffic you must capture with a Funnel Hub.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 297-299

### MM-RB-PDF-007
- **statement:** "Traffic Temperature: Cold → Warm → Hot determines what language and funnel type to use."
- **context:** Via Eugene Schwartz's awareness levels. Cold traffic is problem-aware, warm is solution-aware, hot is product-aware. Each temperature needs different hooks, bridges, and offers.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 321-324

### MM-RB-PDF-008
- **statement:** "Producer vs Consumer mindset for social media."
- **context:** Entrepreneurs must approach every platform as producers—studying Dream 100 patterns, creating content, building distribution. Consuming is wasted time unless it is research.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 155

### MM-RB-PDF-009
- **statement:** "Interest-Based Congregations vs Search-Based Congregations."
- **context:** Two types of places where dream customers gather. Interest-based: social groups, forums, fan pages (scrollers). Search-based: Google, YouTube keywords (searchers). Need different strategies for each.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 53-54

### MM-RB-PDF-010
- **statement:** "Butterfly Marketing: Tiny changes in marketing can produce huge compounding results."
- **context:** Via Mike Filsaime. Small viral mechanisms (badges, refer-a-friend, gamification) can compound into massive growth when the copulation rate exceeds 1.0.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 331-332

### MM-RB-PDF-011
- **statement:** "Integration Marketing: Don't just buy a one-time ad—integrate into your Dream 100's ongoing sales process."
- **context:** Via Mark Joyner. Instead of a single email blast, negotiate to be email #3 in every new subscriber's sequence. You set up the deal once but benefit forever as they grow.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 309

### MM-RB-PDF-012
- **statement:** "Copulation Rate: The viral growth metric. Below 1.0 = dies. At 1.0 = flatlines. Above 1.0 = true viral growth."
- **context:** Via Mark Joyner. To achieve true virality, every person who enters your funnel must refer more than one new person. Extremely hard to sustain but powerful.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 332

### MM-RB-PDF-013
- **statement:** "Prospecting Ads vs Retargeting Ads: Two fundamentally different ad types with different goals and budgets."
- **context:** Prospecting = finding new people (casting a wide net). Retargeting = following up with people who already engaged. Retargeting converts at much higher rates with much lower cost.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 168-175

### MM-RB-PDF-014
- **statement:** "The Funnel Hub: A brand-centric website that organizes all your funnels, captures shadow traffic, and builds credibility."
- **context:** Bridges branding and direct response. Looks like a traditional website for credibility (press, SEO) but strategically funnels visitors into your value ladder.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 295-301

### MM-RB-PDF-015
- **statement:** "Distribution Channels are the real Midas Touch. Sharks invest based on whether they can plug the product into their existing distribution."
- **context:** Shark Tank analysis. Daymond John (retail), Lori Greiner (QVC/TV), Mark Cuban (media)—each invests only where their existing distribution channel applies.
- **page_ref:** PAGE 303-305
