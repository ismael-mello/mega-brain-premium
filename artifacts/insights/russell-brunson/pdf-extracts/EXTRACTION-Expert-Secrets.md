## L1 PHILOSOPHIES (PHI)

### PHI-RB-PDF-001
- **id:** PHI-RB-PDF-001
- **statement:** "People will do almost anything for those who encourage their dreams, justify their failures, allay their fears, confirm their suspicions, and help them throw rocks at their enemies."
- **context:** Blair Warren's One Sentence Persuasion Course, cited by Brunson as the most important 27 words in influence. The 5 elements form the foundation of mass movement leadership.
- **page_ref:** p.33-34

### PHI-RB-PDF-002
- **id:** PHI-RB-PDF-002
- **statement:** "People don't buy improvement offers. They buy new opportunities. An improvement offer asks people to admit failure ('this thing I've been doing isn't working'). A new opportunity gives them a fresh identity with no baggage."
- **context:** Core distinction between why most offers fail vs. why expert-based offers succeed. Ties to STATUS as the primary buying driver.
- **page_ref:** p.59-65

### PHI-RB-PDF-003
- **id:** PHI-RB-PDF-003
- **statement:** "STATUS is the only thing that makes people buy or not buy. The decision is always weighed against the perceived impact on their status — will this increase or decrease my status in the eyes of others?"
- **context:** Brunson's explanation for why improvement offers fail (admitting failure = status decrease) and why new opportunities work (fresh start = status increase potential).
- **page_ref:** p.62-63

### PHI-RB-PDF-004
- **id:** PHI-RB-PDF-004
- **statement:** "If you can make the One Thing believable, all other objections become irrelevant. If the Big Domino falls, all the other little dominos also fall."
- **context:** The Big Domino theory — every sales presentation should focus on a SINGLE belief that, once accepted, makes everything else inevitable. Based on modus ponens logic.
- **page_ref:** p.91-94

### PHI-RB-PDF-005
- **id:** PHI-RB-PDF-005
- **statement:** "Logic doesn't sell. Emotion sells. You can't sell anything with logic — you sell emotionally and justify with logic afterward. True selling is the transference of emotion, not information."
- **context:** Foundation for the Epiphany Bridge concept. People buy based on how they FEEL about the new opportunity, then justify with facts later.
- **page_ref:** p.97-100

### PHI-RB-PDF-006

- **page_ref:** p.145-146

## L2 MENTAL MODELS (MM)

### MM-RB-PDF-001
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-001
- **statement:** "The Prolific Index: Every market has a spectrum from Mainstream (boring, safe) to Crazy (fringe, unacceptable). Your sweet spot is the 'Prolific Zone' — far enough from mainstream to be interesting, close enough to crazy to be edgy, but not so far that you lose credibility."
- **context:** Model for positioning within a niche. Mainstream = ignored. Crazy = dismissed. Prolific Zone = followed. Examples: sungazing community went too far into Crazy Zone.
- **page_ref:** p.36-38

### MM-RB-PDF-002
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-002
- **statement:** "Three Hot Markets → Submarkets → Niches hierarchy. Every business fits within Health, Wealth, or Relationships. Within each are submarkets (e.g., nutrition, real estate, dating). Within submarkets are niches. Your job is to create a NEW niche within an existing submarket."
- **context:** Market selection model. Don't start a new market (no demand). Don't compete in an existing niche (commoditized). Create a new niche within a proven submarket.
- **page_ref:** p.26-28

### MM-RB-PDF-003
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-003
- **statement:** "Opportunity Switch vs. Opportunity Stack. An Opportunity SWITCH replaces what they're currently doing with your new opportunity. An Opportunity STACK adds tools/techniques to their existing new opportunity. First sell the Switch, then Stack over time."
- **context:** Critical distinction for offer sequencing. The initial sale must be a Switch (new vehicle). Follow-up offers can be Stacks (enhancements to the vehicle they already bought into).
- **page_ref:** p.67-69

### MM-RB-PDF-004
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-004
- **statement:** "The Hero's Two Journeys: Every compelling story has TWO parallel journeys — the Journey of Achievement (the external goal) and the Journey of Transformation (the internal change). The audience cares about the transformation 10x more than the achievement."
- **context:** Storytelling model from Michael Hauge. The external achievement is the plot device; the internal transformation is what creates emotional connection.
- **page_ref:** p.113-116

### MM-RB-PDF-005
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-005
- **statement:** "Three categories of false beliefs that block every sale: (1) Vehicle — 'This won't work for me', (2) Internal — 'I can't do this', (3) External — 'Something outside me will prevent my success'. Every objection maps to one of these three."
- **context:** False Belief Pattern model. The Perfect Webinar is structured around breaking one belief from each category (the 3 Secrets).
- **page_ref:** p.131-133, p.139-142

### MM-RB-PDF-006
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-006
- **statement:** "Modus Ponens applied to selling: If A, then B. A is true. Therefore B is true. In sales: If [my new opportunity] is the key to [desired result], and [new opportunity is proven valid], then [prospect must buy]. The Big Domino IS proposition A."
- **context:** Formal logic structure underlying the Big Domino concept. The entire webinar is an argument proving proposition A.
- **page_ref:** p.93-94

### MM-RB-PDF-007
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-007
- **statement:** "The Blue Ocean Strategy applied to niches: Don't compete in a red ocean (crowded niche). Create a blue ocean (new niche/opportunity). But you MUST do it WITHIN a proven submarket — a blue ocean with no demand is just an empty ocean."
- **context:** Brunson's adaptation of Kim and Mauborgne's strategy to the expert/info-product world. New opportunities create blue oceans within validated submarkets.
- **page_ref:** p.28-29

### MM-RB-PDF-008
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-008
- **statement:** "The Kinda Like Bridge: When explaining complex concepts, use 'it's kinda like...' to connect your new idea to something they already understand. Speak at a 3rd to 5th grade reading level to maximize comprehension."
- **context:** Communication model for the Epiphany Bridge. Technical experts fail because they speak at expert level. The 'kinda like' bridge makes any concept accessible.
- **page_ref:** p.101-102

### MM-RB-PDF-009
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-009
- **statement:** "Integration Marketing: Instead of one-time promotions, integrate your offer into the ongoing sales flow of your Dream 100. Put your product on their thank-you pages, in their PS, co-create products. This creates continuous customer streams, not one-time spikes."
- **context:** Mark Joyner's concept adapted by Brunson. Accounts for ~20% of traffic. Changes the Dream 100 relationship from transactional to structural.
- **page_ref:** p.245

### MM-RB-PDF-010
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-010
- **statement:** "Dig Your Well Before You're Thirsty: Build relationships with your Dream 100 BEFORE you need them to promote. The worst time to contact an influencer is the day you need something. The best time is NOW."
- **context:** Harvey Mackay concept applied to Dream 100 relationship building. Three strategies: (1) follow and study them, (2) interview them, (3) help their current project.
- **page_ref:** p.243-244

### MM-RB-PDF-011
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-011
- **statement:** "The Title of Liberty: Every mass movement needs a physical or visual symbol that rallies the tribe. It can be a mini manifesto, a full manifesto, or a video title sequence. The symbol becomes shorthand for the cause."
- **context:** Named after the Book of Mormon story where Captain Moroni tears his coat to create a flag. Examples: Apple's "Think Different", Brunson's "You're just one funnel away."
- **page_ref:** p.46-50

### MM-RB-PDF-012
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-012
- **statement:** "Five Turning Points of Conflict in every Epiphany Bridge story: (1) The initial desire, (2) The first wall/obstacle, (3) The epiphany moment, (4) The plan that emerges, (5) The conflict during execution. These 5 points create the emotional arc."
- **context:** Storytelling structure from the Hero's Two Journeys framework. Each turning point deepens the audience's emotional investment.
- **page_ref:** p.117-119

### MM-RB-PDF-013
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-013
- **statement:** "The 'Why' Drill: To find the internal struggle behind any achievement story, ask 'why?' 5-6 times. Surface: 'I wanted to lose weight.' Level 5: 'I wanted my kids to not be ashamed of me at school.' The deep 'why' is the real story."

- **page_ref:** p.123-124

### MM-RB-PDF-014
- **id:** MM-RB-PDF-014
- **statement:** "Soap Opera Sequence + Perfect Webinar = Email Epiphany Funnel. Each email in the sequence corresponds to one section of the Perfect Webinar (Origin Story, Secret 1, Secret 2, Secret 3, Stack). Each email ends with a hook to the next, like a soap opera cliffhanger."
