## L1: PHILOSOPHIES (18)

- PHI-RB-001: Value Ladder Ascension Philosophy (Every business must have a value ladder; the goal is to move people from low-ticket to high-ticket by providing increasing value at each rung - you earn the right to sell expensive by delivering value first)
- PHI-RB-002: Break Even On Frontend, Profit On Backend (Your initial point-of-sale funnel's goal is to break even on ad costs; all real profit comes from the middle and backend funnels selling to your warm audience)
- PHI-RB-003: Good Profits vs Bad Profits (Inspired by "The Ultimate Question" book - forced continuity, hidden charges, and aggressive upsell chains create bad profits; transparent, value-driven sales create good profits that build long-term businesses)
- PHI-RB-004: Funnels Are Building Blocks / Legos (Every funnel is a modular building block that can be rearranged, swapped, and stacked together - the Black Box funnel is just three core funnels bolted together)
- PHI-RB-005: The One Thing Principle (Every front-end offer, every sales letter, every webinar must be centered around ONE core thing - the one secret, the one trick, the one promise; everything else justifies and supports that one thing)
- PHI-RB-006: Pattern Interrupt Is Mandatory (People are desensitized to millions of ads and emails daily; you MUST interrupt their pattern with bold, shocking, Enquirer-style headlines and imagery to grab attention)
- PHI-RB-007: Re-Skinning Front Ends, Same Backend (Keep your proven upsell/downsell sequence and just create new front-end hooks to attract different segments - a new hook gets new blood in the door, but the backend stays the same)
- PHI-RB-008: Pre-Frame Everything (Every step in your funnel should pre-frame and pre-sell the next step; what you're selling now should prepare them for what comes next)
- PHI-RB-009: Short-Term Profits vs Long-Term Relationship (Sometimes you must sacrifice short-term profits by reducing aggressive upsell chains to protect long-term customer relationships and trust)
- PHI-RB-010: Emotion Sells, Logic Justifies (In high-ticket sales, emotion is closer to the wallet than logic; create the emotional connection first, then bridge with logic - people buy because of hopes, goals, and dreams, not features)
- PHI-RB-011: The Slippery Slope of Commitment (Get small commitments first - shipping address, then credit card; each yes makes the next yes easier, creating a psychological slide toward purchase)
- PHI-RB-012: Every Funnel Type Creates A Pattern; Rotate For Pattern Interrupts (If you do webinars every week, your audience becomes desensitized; rotate between automated webinars, invisible funnels, product launches, and free-plus-shipping to keep them engaged)
- PHI-RB-013: Law Of Reciprocity In Sales (Give massive value first and people feel compelled to pay - the invisible funnel works because people feel guilty receiving value without paying, just like forgetting to pay at a restaurant)
- PHI-RB-014: Don't Start With The Star (The biggest mistake in sales videos is starting with "Hi, my name is..." - you are NOT the most exciting part of your video; pattern interrupt, core desires, and agitation come BEFORE introducing yourself)
- PHI-RB-015: Webinars Are The Highest-Leverage Selling Skill (Learning to sell on a webinar will make you more money than any other single skill; someone can make $50K-$1M+ in 90 minutes)
- PHI-RB-016: Module 7 First, Million Dollars Before Module 8 (This advanced module should only be touched after making at least $1M from your Perfect Webinar; the foundation comes first)
- PHI-RB-017: Never Be The One On The Phone Selling (Entrepreneurs should NOT be the salesperson for their high-ticket offers; you're too emotionally attached to your product - hire setters and closers)
- PHI-RB-018: Inception In Sales (Your goal is to plant ideas in prospects' minds so they think it was their idea; if they have the epiphany WITH you, they're pre-sold because it becomes their own thought)

## L2: MENTAL MODELS (16)

- MM-RB-001: The 9 Core Funnels Model (3 Frontend: Free+Shipping, Tripwire, Micro-Continuity | 3 Middle: Auto-Webinar, Invisible Funnel, Product Launch | 3 Backend: High-Ticket Application, Black Box, Phone Sales Two-Step)
- MM-RB-002: The Black Box Funnel Model (Three funnels bolted together: Free+Shipping CD -> Invisible Funnel upsell -> Coaching Application backend; demonstrates that every "new" funnel is just building blocks rearranged)
- MM-RB-003: Point-Of-Sale Funnel vs Warm-Traffic Funnel (Point-of-sale = the initial funnel for cold traffic where you break even; warm-traffic funnel = what you sell AFTER they know/like/trust you for real profit)
- MM-RB-004: The Script-To-Funnel Matching Model (Who-What-Why-How = free+shipping/squeeze pages up to $7 | Star-Story-Solution = $17-$197 products | Perfect Webinar = $500-$2,500 | High Ticket Two-Step = $5K-$25K+)
- MM-RB-005: Cold Traffic vs Warm Traffic Messaging (Cold traffic = ugly, bold, National Enquirer-style direct response to grab attention | Warm traffic = branding, attractive character, nice design, relationship building)
- MM-RB-006: The Upsell Logic Triangle (Three types of upsells that convert: 1) The Next Thing they need, 2) The Bucket Offer - they got one scoop, sell the whole bucket, 3) Faster & Better - usually software/tools)
- MM-RB-007: Star-Story-Solution Framework (Every sales letter has three parts: Star = the attractive character, Story = the journey that led to the discovery, Solution = your product)
- MM-RB-008: The Homework Page Pre-Frame (Application funnels use a homework page between application and phone call; its purpose is to create emotional connection and pre-frame the prospect for the sales call)
- MM-RB-009: Setter-Closer Two-Step Model (High ticket sales use two people: a Setter who qualifies and builds emotion, and a Closer who handles the four commitments and takes payment - never be the entrepreneur on the phone)
- MM-RB-010: The Four Email Segments of Automated Webinars (Subscribers who registered | Showed-up attendees | Watched-full but didn't buy | No-shows who missed entirely - each gets different email sequences)
- MM-RB-011: Guarantee Escalation Model (Bottom of value ladder = crazy, bold guarantees to get people in the door | Top of value ladder = minimal guarantees because trust is already built and you invest your time)
- MM-RB-012: The Stick Figure Sales Model (Heart = emotion, Brain = logic, Wallet = close to emotion; in high-ticket sales, the wallet is closer to the heart than the brain - sell emotionally first)
- MM-RB-013: Congruency Chain (Subject line = link text = landing page headline = ad headline; using the same language throughout dramatically increases click rates and opt-in rates)
- MM-RB-014: Product Launch 4-Video Model (Video 1: Wow & How | Video 2: Transformational Education | Video 3: Ownership Experience | Video 4: The Offer - released 3-5 days apart to build hype)
- MM-RB-015: Good Profits vs Bad Profits Decision Filter (Before adding any monetization tactic, ask: "Is this good profit or bad profit?" Bad profits = forced continuity, hidden charges, aggressive downsell chains that anger customers)
- MM-RB-016: The Attractive Character Introduction Sequence (Pattern interrupt -> Core desire questions -> Agitate past failures -> Introduce one big thing -> THEN introduce yourself as the attractive character)

## L3: HEURISTICS (32)

- HEUR-RB-001: 10x Price Increase On First Upsell (If front-end is $5, first upsell should be about $50; if $10, upsell to $97 - approximately 10x increase from frontend to first upsell)
- HEUR-RB-002: Max 1 Upsell + 1 Downsell Per Funnel (Russell moved from 6 upsells/downsells to typically just 1 upsell and 1 downsell to protect customer experience - going beyond this hurts long-term relationships)
- HEUR-RB-003: 30% Webinar Show-Up Rate Is Good (If 1,000 people register, getting 300 to actually show up is a strong result for free webinars)
- HEUR-RB-004: 85% Show-Up Rate On Invisible Funnels (Because people put credit card down, invisible funnels get ~85% show-up rate vs ~30% for free webinars - nearly 3x the audience to sell to)
- HEUR-RB-005: ~10% Cancellation Rate On Invisible Funnels (Of 550 people on first invisible funnel, only ~50 canceled = ~90% kept and paid; most non-resulters blame themselves and don't cancel)
- HEUR-RB-006: Webinars Best For $500-$2,500 Products (Middle of value ladder products are best sold through webinars - you don't need a salesperson, just the webinar script)
- HEUR-RB-007: Under 3 Months Average Membership Stick Rate (Average membership site stick rate in internet marketing and weight loss is under 3 months; micro-continuity with a finite end date dramatically improves this)
- HEUR-RB-008: 50% Of Sales From "Watch Replay Now" Option (Testing showed approximately 50% of automated webinar sales came from people who chose to watch "yesterday's replay right now" instead of registering for a future date)
- HEUR-RB-009: Upsell Videos Should Be 3-5 Minutes Max (OTO/upsell videos should never exceed 3-5 minutes; a 20-minute upsell video loses tons of people because they already said yes - just push them gently to the next thing)
- HEUR-RB-010: 3-4 Word Headlines For Cold Traffic (National Enquirer principle: cold traffic headlines should be 3-4 words maximum - "Weird nerve pain miracle" not an 18-word headline)
- HEUR-RB-011: Eyes In Images Increase Conversion (Images with eyes looking at the viewer increase click-through rates; National Enquirer covers always feature eyeballs because humans are drawn to them)
- HEUR-RB-012: Inverted/Negative Images Boost CTR (Taking a normal image and inverting it to negative dramatically increases click-through rates on banner ads and Facebook because the weirdness creates pattern interrupt)
- HEUR-RB-013: $47-$97 Sweet Spot For Invisible Funnel Billing (In the make-money space, no drop-off bumping from $47 to $97 post-webinar billing; two payments of $97 confused the story and didn't work)
- HEUR-RB-014: Invisible Funnel Training Should Be 2-4 Hours (The invisible funnel webinar/training should be 2 to 4 hours of pure teaching with no selling during the content - give as much value as possible)
- HEUR-RB-015: Product Launch Videos Released 3-5 Days Apart (Each of the four product launch videos should be released 3-5 days after the previous one to build anticipation and hype)
- HEUR-RB-016: "When They're Gone, They're Gone" Universal Urgency (When you don't have specific scarcity, use vague but honest urgency: "when they're gone, they're gone" - works without lying)
- HEUR-RB-017: 93% Compliance With Any "Because" Reason (Research shows 93% of people comply with a request if you give ANY reason using the word "because" - even ridiculous reasons work)
- HEUR-RB-018: Bottom Value Ladder = Crazy Guarantees; Top = Minimal (Free+shipping: "keep the book, we'll refund your $5" | $25K coaching: 3-day contractual cancellation window only)
- HEUR-RB-019: Contact Leads Within 24-48 Hours (Print applications daily and call immediately; leads that sit for two weeks go cold and become nearly worthless for high-ticket conversion)
- HEUR-RB-020: First 5-15 Minutes Reveals The Buyer (In high-ticket phone sales, within the first 5-15 minutes of conversation, a skilled setter knows whether the person will become a buyer based on emotional engagement)
- HEUR-RB-021: Downsell Options: Payment Plan, Format Shift, Different Offer (Three downsell strategies: 1) Payment plan of the same offer, 2) Digital version instead of physical, 3) Completely different offer - NEVER just discount the same offer incrementally)
- HEUR-RB-022: Never Discount Incrementally On Downsells (Dropping price $10, then $20, then $30 on the same offer destroys credibility and faith - this is horrible for customer relationships)
- HEUR-RB-023: Continuity Should NOT Be In Point-Of-Sale Funnel (Russell's personal rule: never tie continuity/recurring billing into the initial point-of-sale funnel; it creates bad profits and angry customers who didn't know they were on continuity)
- HEUR-RB-024: Perfect Webinar Is 60-90 Minutes Total (The perfect webinar runs approximately 60-90 minutes: ~15 min intro/rapport, ~45 min content with 3 secrets, ~15-30 min close/stack/urgency)
- HEUR-RB-025: Three Secrets Structure For Webinar Content (Teach exactly 3 secrets/modules in your perfect webinar - not 2, not 5 - three secrets gives enough value without overwhelming)
- HEUR-RB-026: $1M+ In First 90 Days With Automated Webinar (DotCom Secrets Labs automated webinar did $1M in sales in first 90 days, then over $3M over 3 years running continuously)
- HEUR-RB-027: 20% Buy 6X Bundle On Upsell (When bundling products on upsell - e.g., offering 6 bottles instead of 1 - approximately 20% of buyers take the larger bundle)
- HEUR-RB-028: High Ticket Setter Call Takes 1.5-2 Hours (The full high-ticket two-step script process generally takes about 1.5 to 2 hours to go through the complete setter + closer sequence)
- HEUR-RB-029: 70-75% Profit Margin On High Ticket Sales (With a properly built high-ticket funnel and 2-3 person call center, you can consistently bring in $50-70K/week at 70-75% profit margin)
- HEUR-RB-030: Three Sales Staff Can Outperform Sixty (Russell's 3-person team with the right script nets more revenue than their previous 60-person call center did)
- HEUR-RB-031: Free+Shipping Refund Rates Are Negligible (Very few people ever ask for a refund on a free+shipping offer - the $5 shipping is too small to bother requesting back)
- HEUR-RB-032: Subject Line = Link Text = Headline Increases Conversions (Making the email link text match the subject line dramatically increased click rates; making the landing page headline match both increased opt-in rates)

- FW-RB-013: Invisible Funnel Script - Magic Bullet Registration Page (Raise questions about their frustrations -> Introduce the magic bullet promise -> Introduce yourself -> Point out their frustrations -> Explain the webinar -> Risk reversal: come for free, pay only if you love it)
- FW-RB-014: Invisible Funnel Script - Teaching Webinar (Hook + tell what they'll learn -> Content block 1: parable/story + concept + example -> Repeat for all content -> Recap everything learned -> Tie to tangible value -> Ask if it was worth it -> Soft close to backend offer using What-Why-How)
- FW-RB-015: High-Ticket Two-Step Sales Script (SETTER: Introduction -> Qualifying questions about business -> The Blast: share excitement about program -> Posture: why yours is different -> Probe: find pain, goals, credit availability -> Share commitments -> Pass to closer | CLOSER: Time commitment -> Decision maker? -> Resources available? -> Knowledge/what's included -> Close the sale)
- FW-RB-016: The Four Commitments Close (Closer's framework: 1) Do you have the TIME? 2) Are you the DECISION MAKER? 3) Do you have the RESOURCES? 4) Do you want the KNOWLEDGE we provide? -> Then sign up and close)
- FW-RB-017: The Enquirer Interrupt Framework (Study National Enquirer headlines -> 3-4 words max -> Weird/bold imagery with eyes -> Match banner/ad headline to squeeze page headline -> Match email subject to link text to landing page headline)
- FW-RB-018: The Consulting Application Funnel (Video with case studies of success stories -> Apply Now button -> Application with qualifying questions -> Homework page with emotional pre-frame video that makes people cry -> Phone call next day with setter who leverages the emotional pre-frame)
- FW-RB-019: Downsell Strategy Framework (Upsell rejected -> Option A: Payment plan of same offer | Option B: Shift fulfillment format (physical to digital) | Option C: Completely different offer | NEVER: Incremental price drops on same offer)
- FW-RB-020: The Value Ladder Funnel Map Process (Step 1: Map your value ladder with price points | Step 2: Design point-of-sale funnel for bottom of ladder | Step 3: Design warm-traffic funnel for middle/top of ladder | Step 4: Build in ClickFunnels | Step 5: Drive traffic)
- FW-RB-021: Micro-Continuity vs Membership Site Design (Finite program: 6-8 weeks, weekly unlocks, specific end date, graduation -> Much higher stick rates than open-ended membership sites because there's a goal, progress, and completion)
- FW-RB-022: Post-Selling Framework (After first CTA, speak to people who already ordered: "For those who just signed up, here's what to expect..." -> Creates FOMO in non-buyers who feel excluded -> Then switch to takeaway: "Whether you buy or not, I'll be fine - this is about YOU")
- FW-RB-023: Bucket Offer Upsell Framework (Sell one module/piece as front-end -> Upsell: "That was 1 of 8 presentations from our event, want access to all of them?" -> Customer got one scoop, now offer the whole bucket)
- FW-RB-024: Credit-As-Leverage Framework for High Ticket (Explain to prospects that credit is a tool like a real estate investor uses bank money -> Compare: $100K in student loans vs $2K coaching investment -> Most non-entrepreneurs don't realize credit can be leveraged for business investment)
