## L1: PHILOSOPHIES (12)

- PHI-RB-001: Funnelology Is A Science ("Funnelology is the science of funnels" - understanding the building blocks, phases, and psychology behind how funnels work is a learnable, repeatable discipline, not guesswork)
- PHI-RB-002: Never Build Without A Blueprint ("You wouldn't go build a house without a blueprint" - before building any business or funnel, first find what has worked in the past, find something successful, and model it)
- PHI-RB-003: Modeling Over Inventing (Learned from Tony Robbins: "If you want to achieve success, all you need to do is find a way to model those who have already succeeded" - funnel hacking can shave 5-15 years off your learning curve)
- PHI-RB-004: Funnels Are A Value Ladder (Funnels are not standalone - they are a value ladder system where communication funnels bridge people from funnel one to funnel two to funnel three, ascending them through increasing value)
- PHI-RB-005: Funnel Stacking Glues Everything Together (The three core funnels in a business work together: front-end funnel leads to communication funnel/emails, which leads to second funnel, then more follow-up, then to the highest-level offer - the emails are the glue)
- PHI-RB-006: A Buyer Is A Buyer Is A Buyer (From Dan Kennedy - the person willing to pull out their credit card is the most valuable person in the world; once you know they are a buyer, you market to them completely differently than a non-buyer)
- PHI-RB-007: All Clicks Are Not Created Equal (Learned from Mark Joyner - the pre-frame through which someone arrives at your page completely changes their behavior; the same click from different contexts produces radically different conversion outcomes)
- PHI-RB-008: Amateurs Focus On The Front End ("Amateurs focus on the front" - you will lose money on free-plus-shipping front-end offers no matter what; the question is just how much you are willing to lose to acquire a buyer you can ascend)
- PHI-RB-009: Give Away Your Best Stuff For Free (The counterintuitive move of giving away your best product for free creates maximum goodwill - "if we just completely over-delivered, they'd probably want more" - this is how you attract your dream clients)
- PHI-RB-010: Free Is The Most Magical Word In Marketing (The word "free" creates irrational behavior shifts - Predictably Irrational chocolate experiment showed that making something free vs. one cent shifted purchasing from 20% to 80%; use this in your bait)
- PHI-RB-011: The Tip Of The Iceberg (What you see of a competitor's business is just the tip of the iceberg; the real money-making happens in all the hidden steps below - upsells, downsells, email sequences, backend offers - that most people never see)
- PHI-RB-012: There Is No Such Thing As An Advertising Budget (Once you get a funnel to break even - put a dollar in and get a dollar back - your advertising budget is infinite; you spend Monday, merchant account Tuesday, bank account Wednesday, and start over)

## L2: MENTAL MODELS (11)

- MM-RB-001: Traffic Temperature Model (Three temperatures of traffic based on Gene Schwartz's awareness levels: HOT = product aware, know you and want to buy; WARM = solution aware, know the category but not you; COLD = problem aware or unaware, only know they have a pain. The page/funnel you build must match the temperature of traffic you are driving)
- MM-RB-002: The Pre-Frame Bridge (The context through which someone arrives at your content shapes their entire experience. Like a speaker introduction at an event: the same speaker with a warm intro crushes it, with a cold intro bombs. One word change on a squeeze page - "finish" vs "get started" - changed conversion and downstream behavior dramatically)
- MM-RB-003: The Iceberg Model Of Business (What's visible to competitors is just the tip; all the underground hidden steps - upsells, downsells, email sequences, backend funnels, phone sales - are what make the offer actually profitable. You cannot replicate success by copying only the visible surface)
- MM-RB-004: The Dam Analogy For Price Points (Your front-end price is like a dam in a river; a $97 or $197 dam blocks most people from flowing through your funnel. Remove the dam by making the first offer free-plus-shipping, and "tons of people came in" - transforming the entire company)
- MM-RB-005: The Dating Analogy For Customer Relationships (Funnels mirror dating: introduction = pre-frame, getting phone number = qualifying subscriber, first kiss = qualifying buyer, kissing back = hyperactive buyer, subsequent dates = aging the relationship, proposal = changing the selling environment)
- MM-RB-006: Porcupine In Heat Buyer Psychology (Buyers experience windows of intense buying motivation - "buyers in heat" - where they will buy everything available to solve their pain. This window is short; you must identify and serve hyperactive buyers immediately through upsells before the moment passes)
- MM-RB-007: Freemium To Premium Transition (From Survival Life's credit card knife funnel: take someone from a free physical product to paid membership by bribing them with a premium version of the same type of product, bundled with membership access)
- MM-RB-008: The Itch Has Been Scratched (When someone buys a product on a specific topic, in their mind that problem is solved even though they haven't consumed it yet. Upselling more of the same topic fails because "the itch is scratched." You must sell the NEXT STEP in the process, not more of the same)
- MM-RB-009: Open Loop vs Closed Loop Selling (Saying "your order is complete, thanks" closes the mental loop and kills upsell conversions. Saying "wait, your order is not yet complete" keeps the loop open and dramatically increases conversion on the next offer)
- MM-RB-010: Funnel Hacking As Competitive Intelligence (Treat competitor funnels as free market research: buy their products, track their email sequences, screenshot every page, note every price point. You get years of their testing and optimization for free in a week)
- MM-RB-011: Comfort Zone Sales Environment Theory (People behind computers are in their comfort zone and resistant to high-ticket sales. Changing the selling environment - events, phone calls, in-person - removes comfort zone resistance and enables $25K-$100K+ sales that are "pretty much impossible" online)

## L3: HEURISTICS (18)

- HEUR-RB-001: 1% Conversion Rule For High-Ticket Front Ends (Out of 100 visitors, a $197 front-end product averages about 1% conversion rate, yielding $197 per 100 visitors)
- HEUR-RB-002: 8% Conversion Rule For Free-Plus-Shipping (Out of 100 visitors, a free-plus-shipping offer averages about 8% conversion rate; combined with upselling the same $197 product, this yields 8 buyers and roughly 2x the revenue per 100 visitors vs. selling the $197 directly)
- HEUR-RB-003: Facebook Email Subscriber Value - $80 vs $1 (Jeremy Shoemaker found that a subscriber who uses their Facebook email address averages $80/year in value, while a non-Facebook email averages only $1/year)
- HEUR-RB-004: 7 Touches Before Purchase (It takes an average person 7 experiences or touches with you before they feel comfortable enough to buy; squeeze pages enable this follow-up sequence)
- HEUR-RB-005: Quiz Pre-Frame Doubles Conversion (Glen Ledwell's Mind Movies went from 1.5% conversion on Facebook to more than double by adding a simple quiz before the webinar registration - the quiz "really does nothing at all" but gets engagement and micro-commitments)
- HEUR-RB-006: Shipping Price Threshold - Under $5 to Under $10 (For free-plus-shipping offers: keep shipping under $5 for best results, under $10 is acceptable; above $10 conversion drops significantly. Russell uses $4.95 for DVDs and $9.95 for books)
- HEUR-RB-007: 23% Increase From One Word Change (Changing step 3 label from "get started" to "finish" increased front-end squeeze page conversion by ~23%, but dramatically decreased next-page conversion because it closed the mental loop)
- HEUR-RB-008: Lift Letter Effect - 2-3x Response Increase (In direct mail, adding a lift letter - a one-page endorsement from the list owner placed on top of the sales letter - increases response 2-3x. Email functions as the digital equivalent of a lift letter)
- HEUR-RB-009: Upsell "Next Step" vs "More Of Same" Conversion (Upselling the next logical step in the process converts dramatically better than upselling more of the same topic. Russell's traffic upsell after a conversion book hit 23% conversion instantly; every "more of the same" upsell bombed)
- HEUR-RB-010: Print On Demand Testing Cost - ~$6 Per Unit (Use Disk Delivered for print-on-demand testing at $5.95 US / $7.95 international per unit, with $50 setup; only order in bulk once you've proven the offer works and are selling 1,000+ per month)
- HEUR-RB-011: Funnel Break-Even Iteration Timeline (Russell's supplement Nerve Cell took almost a year to get the funnel working. January: $20K revenue. March (2 months later): $300K revenue. Next month projection: $500K. The scaling happens fast once break-even is achieved)
- HEUR-RB-012: Webinar Best Day - Thursdays (Russell's webinars convert best on Thursdays; different markets may have different optimal days based on what competitors have already trained audiences to expect)
- HEUR-RB-013: Funnel Hacks Webinar Revenue - $2M+ From 3 Pages (Russell's funnel hacks webinar generated over $2 million from just three pages: registration page, confirmation page/welcome video, and the webinar itself)
- HEUR-RB-014: 130K Buyers In One Month From Free-Plus-Shipping (Ryan Deiss and Perry Belcher sold 130,000 free credit card knives in month one via Facebook only - no affiliates - creating 130K new buyers for their funnel)
- HEUR-RB-015: Survival Business From $30K/mo to $1M/mo (Before adding the free-plus-shipping front end, the survival company was doing $20-30K/month and could not break through; after adding the credit card knife, they scaled to over $1 million/month with 350,000+ buyers)
- HEUR-RB-016: Print Newsletter at $97/Month Capped at 250 Subscribers (Russell's $97/month print newsletter with split test results stalled at ~250 subscribers despite heavy promotion; converting the same content to a free-plus-shipping book generated 6,000-7,000 buyers)
- HEUR-RB-017: Only 1 Out of 10,000 Noticed Blank DVDs (Russell shipped over 10,000 DVDs with no content actually burned on them; only one person ever called to complain - proving most people never consume the product, they buy for the promise)
- HEUR-RB-018: High-Ticket Funnel Structure - 3 Steps (Video testimonial/case study page -> Application page with video of case studies playing while they fill out form -> Homework page with personal/emotional video from the founder explaining why they care)

## L4: FRAMEWORKS (18)

- FW-RB-001: The Three Core Funnels Stack (Russell's three core funnels and how they connect: 1) Book Funnel (front-end) -> Communication Funnel/Emails -> 2) Webinar Funnel (mid-tier) -> Communication Funnel/Emails -> 3) Inner Circle/High-Ticket (backend). Each funnel moves people up the value ladder)
- FW-RB-002: The Seven Phases Of A Funnel / Lifeline Of A Lead (Phase 1: Traffic Temperature - determine hot/warm/cold; Phase 2: Pre-Frame Bridge - warm them up before the offer; Phase 3: Qualify Subscribers - get email via squeeze page; Phase 4: Qualify Buyers - get credit card via low-barrier offer; Phase 5: Identify Buyers In Heat - hyperactive buyers via upsells/downsells; Phase 6: Age and Ascend the Relationship - follow-up, webinars, new offers; Phase 7: Change the Selling Environment - events, phone, face-to-face for high-ticket)
- FW-RB-003: The 23 Building Blocks Of A Funnel (6 categories of building blocks, one for each funnel phase: Pre-Frame [7 types] -> Qualify Subscribers [7 types] -> Qualify Buyers [5 types] -> Identify Buyers In Heat [4 types] -> Age & Ascend [multiple types] -> Change Environment [multiple types]. You pick one building block from each category to assemble your funnel)
- FW-RB-004: Seven Pre-Frame Building Blocks (1. Quiz, 2. Article/News/Blog, 3. Video (YouTube), 4. Email/Lift Letter, 5. Presale Page, 6. Third-party testimonial blog, 7. Review site)
- FW-RB-005: Seven Qualify-Subscribers Building Blocks (1. Squeeze Page, 2. Pop-up, 3. Squeeze Pop (button-triggered popup), 4. Free-Plus-Shipping two-step, 5. Webinar Registration, 6. Free Account, 7. Exit Pop)
- FW-RB-006: Five Qualify-Buyers Building Blocks (1. Free-Plus-Shipping, 2. Trial offer ($1 trial), 3. Tripwire ($7 report), 4. Self-Liquidating Offer (~$37 product), 5. Straight Sale (~$100+ product))
- FW-RB-007: Four Buyers-In-Heat Building Blocks (1. One-Time Offer / Upsell page, 2. Downsell page, 3. Recommend someone else's product, 4. Bump on order form - "for an extra $37, add this to your order")
- FW-RB-008: Age-And-Ascend Building Blocks (Product launches, Live webinars, Automated webinars, Tele-seminars, New product offers, Various funnel types: invisible funnel, black box funnel, micro-continuity funnel)
- FW-RB-009: Change-Environment Building Blocks (Live events/seminars, Phone sales, Application pages, Face-to-face selling - used when selling $15K-$100K+ programs)
- FW-RB-010: Funnel Hacking Process - 5 Steps (Step 1: Find someone successful in your market or adjacent market; Step 2: Buy their product and go through their entire funnel; Step 3: Sketch out the funnel - every page, price point, upsell, email sequence; Step 4: Map out your version using their blueprint; Step 5: Build it in your funnel builder)
- FW-RB-011: The 100 Visitor Test (Compare two approaches with 100 visitors: Option A = sell $197 product directly at 1% conversion = $197 revenue and 1 buyer; Option B = free-plus-shipping at 8% conversion then upsell $197 = ~2x revenue and 8 buyers. Option B wins on both revenue AND buyer acquisition)
- FW-RB-012: Tripwire Funnel Structure (Free-plus-shipping front-end -> Upsell 1: next logical step product -> Upsell 2: next logical step or payment plan on previous -> Potential downsell if they decline. Key: each upsell is the NEXT step, not more of the same)
- FW-RB-013: Perfect Webinar Funnel Structure (Registration page -> Confirmation page with welcome video and homework -> Indoctrination email sequence (3 videos between registration and webinar) -> Live/automated webinar with stack and close -> Post-webinar follow-up for non-buyers)
- FW-RB-014: High-Ticket Coaching Funnel Structure (Page 1: Video testimonial/case study from a successful client -> Page 2: Application form with background video of multiple case study testimonials -> Page 3: Homework page with personal/emotional video from founder + assignments -> Phone close by sales team)
- FW-RB-015: The Breakeven Funnel Formula (Build a funnel where $1 in ads = $1 back out. If less than breakeven, add an upsell or tweak elements until you reach breakeven. Once at breakeven, your list grows for free and all backend sales are pure profit. Then split test to push above breakeven)
- FW-RB-016: Front-End Bait Engineering ("What's the one sexiest, most cool thing of the entire program?" - pull out that ONE thing and make it the free-plus-shipping offer. Not the whole program, just the single most compelling element that makes your dream client say "I need that")
- FW-RB-017: Repurpose Sales Tools As Bait (Take a sales webinar that converted well -> transcribe it into a free book OR burn it to a free DVD -> use as a new free-plus-shipping offer targeting a fresh audience. Same content, new hook, new format, new audience)
- FW-RB-018: Customer-Financed Direct Mail (Use free-plus-shipping orders to finance sending direct mail sales pieces to buyers. The $9.95 shipping covers the physical product + a 4-page sales letter for your $25,000 program included in the same package. Buyers finance your direct mail campaign to them)

## L5: METHODOLOGIES (9)

- MET-RB-001: Funnel Hacking Methodology (1. Identify a successful funnel in your market or transferable market; 2. Buy the product and go through every step; 3. Set up a Gmail filter to capture all their emails into a dedicated folder; 4. Screenshot every page - landing page, order form, upsell, downsell, thank you page; 5. Note price points, offers, headlines, copy length, video presence; 6. Sketch the funnel on a whiteboard with all knowns documented; 7. Create your version using their proven structure as the blueprint; 8. Build it and test)
- MET-RB-002: Building Block Assembly Method (1. Determine your traffic source and its temperature; 2. Select one Pre-Frame building block from the 7 options; 3. Select one Qualify Subscribers building block from the 7 options; 4. Select one Qualify Buyers building block from the 5 options; 5. Select one or more Buyers-In-Heat building blocks from the 4 options; 6. Design your Age & Ascend strategy; 7. Design your Change Environment strategy; 8. Connect all blocks into a complete funnel)
- MET-RB-003: Upsell Sequencing Method - "Next Step" Logic (1. Identify the ONE thing your front-end offer solves; 2. Ask "now that this itch is scratched, what's the NEXT thing they need?"; 3. Create upsell 1 around that next step; 4. Ask again "now that's handled, what's the NEXT thing?"; 5. Create upsell 2 around that. Example: Conversion book -> Traffic product -> Sales product. Weight loss: Diet guide -> Exercise program -> Supplements)
- MET-RB-004: Free-Plus-Shipping Launch Methodology (1. Identify the ONE thing from your program; 2. Create a physical product - book, DVD, CD - around that one thing; 3. Use print-on-demand (Disk Delivered ~$6/unit) to test without inventory risk; 4. Price shipping at $4.95-$9.95; 5. Drive Facebook traffic with an iPhone video of you holding the product; 6. Test the offer; 7. If it works, scale; if it bombs, change the hook/headline and retest with the same content; 8. Once proven, order in bulk to reduce costs)
- MET-RB-005: Breakeven Funnel Optimization Process (1. Build initial funnel and drive traffic; 2. If losing money, add an upsell and test again; 3. If still losing, tweak the front-end offer type - switch from self-liquidating to free-plus-shipping, or tripwire to straight sale; 4. Split test individual elements; 5. Continue until $1 in = $1 out; 6. If initial test breaks even, celebrate - you haven't even optimized yet; 7. Make tweaks to push above breakeven; 8. Scale by cranking up ad spend with no budget limit)
- MET-RB-006: Pre-Frame Engineering For Traffic Temperature (1. Identify where your traffic is coming from; 2. Classify as hot/warm/cold; 3. For HOT traffic (JV partners, your own list): short pre-frame, product-specific messaging, direct to offer; 4. For WARM traffic (Facebook, content sites): solution-aware messaging, quiz or article pre-frame; 5. For COLD traffic (broad display, cold audiences): problem-aware messaging, extensive pre-frame with blog/quiz/video/presale page before the offer)
- MET-RB-007: Quiz Funnel Pre-Frame Method (1. Create a headline: "Take this 30-second quiz to reveal your #1 [problem]"; 2. Create 3-5 multiple choice questions that engage but don't need to actually segment; 3. Show a progress bar (step 1, step 2, step 3); 4. Include psychological pre-frame elements in questions - e.g., "How did you hear about us? Dr. Oz / Biggest Loser / Jillian Michaels"; 5. After quiz, require email opt-in to see results; 6. Results page sells them on the next step - webinar registration or offer)
- MET-RB-008: Funnel Stacking Communication Method (1. Build front-end funnel (e.g., free book); 2. Create follow-up email sequence introducing the Attractive Character; 3. After relationship building, transition emails to sell the mid-tier offer (e.g., webinar); 4. After webinar sequence, create new email sequence leading to high-ticket application; 5. Each communication funnel bridges one funnel to the next, moving people up the value ladder)
- MET-RB-009: Bait-To-Congregation Targeting Method (1. Identify your dream client avatar; 2. Create free-plus-shipping bait specifically designed to attract ONLY that avatar; 3. Go to Facebook and find congregations - fan pages of competitors, related companies, gurus in your market; 4. Target each congregation individually with your bait; 5. Pull them from competitor audiences into your funnel. Example: MLM bait -> target Vemma distributors, then ViSalus distributors, then Manatech distributors, one by one)
