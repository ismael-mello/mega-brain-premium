# TEMA: Perfect Webinar - The Complete Selling System

[[HOME|<- Home]] | [[MOC-TEMAS|Temas]] | [[MOC-PESSOAS|Pessoas]] | [[MOC-AGENTES|Agentes]]

> **Em resumo:** The Perfect Webinar is Russell Brunson's master framework for selling $500-$2,500 products via presentation. Structure: 15 min intro + 45 min content (3 Secrets addressing 3 False Beliefs) + 15-30 min close with The Stack. The Stack is NON-NEGOTIABLE -- total stated value must be 10x price. Trial Closes embedded throughout. Big Domino = single belief that makes all objections irrelevant. 10-15% close rate live, 50% from stage. One-third of all sales come in last 6 hours. Automated version: 50% of sales from "watch replay now" option.

## Filosofia Central

The Perfect Webinar is not a webinar -- it is a universal selling system. It works on stage, on Facebook Live, in email sequences, and in automated funnels. The core insight: selling is about creating BELIEF, not presenting information. If you try to teach, you kill the sale. Stories create belief; instruction does not.

## Armadilhas

### Teaching Mode
The number one killer of webinar sales. The moment you switch from storytelling to instruction, belief drops and wallets close. 60% story, 40% content. ^[PHI-RB-018] ^[HEUR-RB-071]

### Skipping The Stack
Russell once bombed on stage because he forgot the Stack. The Stack is literally non-negotiable. ^[PHI-RB-057]

### Video on Registration Pages
Russell has never tested a video on a registration page that helped. Always hurts conversion. ^[HEUR-RB-063]

### Multiple Big Dominos
One belief per sales message. Multiple beliefs = bombs. Find THE ONE domino. ^[HEUR-RB-057] ^[PHI-RB-044]

### Creating New Webinars Too Early
You are not allowed to do anything else until that presentation has made you at least a million dollars. ^[PHI-RB-058]

### Webinar Diagnostic ^[HEUR-RB-101]

If your webinar fails, it's one of exactly 3 reasons:
1. You picked a bad market
2. You built an improvement offer instead of a new opportunity
3. You slipped into teaching mode instead of creating an environment for change

All are pre-webinar strategic errors, not presentation errors. ^[Expert Secrets p.232]
