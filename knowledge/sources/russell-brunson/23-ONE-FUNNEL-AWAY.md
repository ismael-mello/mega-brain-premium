## L2 — MODELOS MENTAIS (Mental Models)

**MM-RB-OFA-001**
**Título:** Who Not How (Dan Sullivan Framework)
**Conteúdo:** "The concept is: Who Not How." When you get stuck on a "how" (how do I build a logo? how do I edit video?), you get sucked into a rabbit hole of learning that derails your vision. The alternative: ask "who already knows how to do this?" and find that person. Stop trying to learn every skill — find the who that already has the skill.
**Mecanismo:** Vision → encounter a "how" → rabbit hole of learning → derailed → OR → vision → encounter a "how" → ask "who can do this?" → delegate/hire → stay on path to vision
**Contexto:** Pre-Training Day 3 "Who Not How" — biggest sticking point Russell sees across 15 years of coaching
**Aplicação:** When you feel stuck on implementation, immediately ask: "Who already does this?" before spending time learning it yourself.
**Fonte:** `transcription_04-PT-M3-_Who_Not_How.txt`

**MM-RB-OFA-002**
**Título:** Problems Create Product Sequences (The Infinite Game)
**Conteúdo:** Every offer solves an initial problem. But solving that problem creates new follow-up problems — which become opportunities for more products. "This is a never-ending cool game where they continue to have additional good problems." Customers who solve Problem A now have Problem B, which you can also solve.
**Mecanismo:** Solve initial problem → customer achieves partial success → reveals next problem → new product opportunity → repeat indefinitely
**Contexto:** Week 1 Day 3 — Offer Sequencing
**Aplicação:** Map out your customer's entire journey. Each product should solve one problem AND create awareness of the next problem you can solve.
**Fonte:** `transcription_11-Week1_Day_3_from_ClickFunnels_on_Vimeo.txt`
**Speaker:** Stephen Larsen (coaching call)

**MM-RB-OFA-003**
**Título:** Product vs Offer — The Price War Trap
**Conteúdo:** "If you have a product, then you're competing with everybody else who also has a product. When everyone's competing with the same thing, then it's a price war down to the bottom very, very quickly." A product is a commodity. An offer is a combination of products, bonuses, guarantees, and context that creates unique perceived value — impossible to price-compare.
**Mecanismo:** Product alone → price comparison → race to bottom. Offer (product + bonuses + guarantee + context) → unique value stack → price becomes irrelevant
**Contexto:** Week 1 Day 2 "The Greatest Showman" — offer creation
**Aplicação:** Never sell a standalone product. Always bundle into an offer with supporting elements that competitors cannot replicate.
**Fonte:** `transcription_10-W1-M2-_Greatest_Showman.txt`

## L4 — FRAMEWORKS (Frameworks)

**FW-RB-OFA-001**
**Título:** OFA Weekly Structure — The 4-Week Framework
**Componentes:**
- Week 1: OFFER — identify, hack, and build your irresistible offer
- Week 2: STORY — develop your Epiphany Bridge story and origin story
- Week 3: HOOK — create compelling hooks for your ads and landing pages
- Week 4: FUNNEL + TRAFFIC — build the funnel and launch traffic
**Lógica:** Reversed from conventional thinking (most teach traffic first). Offer quality determines whether everything else succeeds. Story quality determines conversion. Hook quality determines click-through. Funnel is the delivery mechanism.
**Contexto:** Week 1 Day 1 intro
**Aplicação:** Use this sequence for any new product launch: validate and build offer → craft story → engineer hooks → build funnel → drive traffic.
**Fonte:** `transcription_09-W1-M1-Offer_Hacking.txt`

**FW-RB-OFA-002**
**Título:** The Offer Hacking Research Framework
**Componentes:**
1. Choose your broad market (weight loss, make money, relationships, etc.)
2. Identify 10 competitors currently running paid traffic
3. Opt into their funnels — experience the full sequence as a buyer
4. Document: price point, bonus stack, guarantee, positioning, hook
5. Identify patterns: what do ALL successful offers in this space share?
6. Identify gaps: what do none of them offer that you can?
7. Build your offer incorporating patterns + filling gaps
**Ferramentas:** Amazon (search by category for book ideas = market validation), Google, Facebook Ad Library, ClickFunnels marketplace
**Contexto:** Week 1 Day 1 Offer Hacking mission
**Aplicação:** Mandatory first step for any new offer. Do not skip. 2-4 hours of research saves months of building the wrong thing.
**Fonte:** `transcription_09-W1-M1-Offer_Hacking.txt`

**FW-RB-OFA-003**
**Título:** The Epiphany Bridge Story Framework
**Componentes:**
1. **The Background** — Who were you before? (Establish relatability with your audience's current state)
2. **The Wall** — What wasn't working? What problem were you stuck on?
3. **The Epiphany Moment** — The exact moment you discovered the solution (the "aha")
4. **The New Opportunity** — What changed after the epiphany? What became possible?
5. **The Results** — What happened when you applied the solution?
6. **The Bridge** — "If it worked for me in [my situation], it can work for you in yours"
**Aplicação:** Use in VSLs, webinar openers, email sequences, and social content. Each element of the bridge answers a buyer objection: "Is this person real?" → Background. "Does this work?" → Results. "Will it work for me?" → The Bridge.
**Contexto:** Week 2 Day 2 "Epiphany Bridge"
**Fonte:** `transcription_17-W2-M2-_Epiphany_Bridge.txt`

## L5 — METODOLOGIAS (Methodologies)

**MET-RB-OFA-001**
**Título:** The 30-Day Challenge Structure — OFA Execution Methodology
**Fases:**
- **Pre-Training (Days -7 to 0):** 5 days of belief-building. No tactics. Case studies, failure stories, success stories. Goal: participant believes outcome is achievable before Day 1.
- **Week 1 (Days 1-5):** Offer creation. Offer hacking (Day 1) → product creation (Day 2) → offer sequencing (Day 3) → offer assembly (Days 4-5). Deliverable: complete offer with pricing and bonus stack.
- **Week 2 (Days 6-12):** Story creation. Epiphany Bridge (Day 1) → story selling framework (Day 2) → origin story (Day 3) → story application across funnel (Days 4-5). Deliverable: core story script.
- **Week 3 (Days 13-19):** Hook creation. Deliverable: ad hooks, headline variants, landing page hooks.
- **Week 4 (Days 20-30):** Funnel build + traffic launch. Deliverable: live funnel with first traffic campaign.
**Princípio:** Each week's deliverable is a prerequisite for the next. No skipping.
**Contexto:** Full OFA curriculum structure
**Fonte:** Multiple files across all weeks

**MET-RB-OFA-002**
**Título:** The Offer Hacking-to-Launch Methodology (Week 1 Sequence)
**Passos:**
1. **Offer Hack** (Day 1): Research 10 competitors. Document their full offer stack.
2. **Create Products Fast** (Day 2): Build 3-5 small digital products using speed methods (screen-recorded video, PDF checklist, template). Each solves one specific problem.
3. **Sequence the Offer** (Day 3): Map the customer journey — what problem do they have first? What follows after they solve that? Build product sequence to address each step.
4. **Stack the Offer** (Days 4-5): Combine products into an irresistible offer with: core product + 2-3 bonuses + guarantee + clear transformation promise.
5. **Price** (Day 5): Price based on value of transformation, not cost of production.
**Saída:** A complete, packaged offer ready to be sold before any funnel is built.
**Contexto:** Week 1 Days 1-5

**MET-RB-OFA-003**
**Título:** The Daily Publishing Methodology (Stephen Larsen)
**Passos:**
1. **Choose a platform** where your audience already lives (Facebook, YouTube, podcast, or email)
2. **Publish daily** — content tied to the problem your offer solves
3. **Teach to learn:** After each lesson you consume, create a post/video teaching what you learned
4. **Content formula:** Story → Lesson → Call to Action
5. **Track engagement:** What gets comments and shares = what the market wants more of
6. **Repurpose:** One piece of content → multiple formats (video → transcript → email → social post)
**Frequência:** Daily minimum. Consistency over quality at the start.
**Contexto:** Week 2 Day 1 Stephen Larsen coaching call on publishing

**MET-SL-OFA-001**
**Título:** The Coaching Call Learning Loop (Stephen Larsen's Methodology)
**Passos:**
1. **Before each session:** Ask students "What was your #1 takeaway from yesterday?" — forces recall and public accountability
2. **During session:** Teach concept → apply to student's specific situation → show worked example
3. **After session:** Students immediately teach the concept to someone else (spouse, friend, social post)
4. **Next session:** Begin with takeaway review — closes the loop and seeds retention
**Fundamento:** "One of the ways that you learn really, really fast, really, really easily is by getting a coach and being a coach."
**Contexto:** Stephen Larsen coaching call structure across all 30 days
**Aplicação:** Use this loop in any group coaching program, mastermind, or online course. The teach-back accountability creates 10x better retention than passive consumption.
