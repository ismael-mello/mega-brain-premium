## L1 - PHILOSOPHIES (4)

**FIL-RB-FU-001** | Self-Liquidating Content Philosophy
> "You're going to be spending money to get people to listen, read or watch your content. That's just kind of how the process works. The goal is to self-liquidate. If you spend a dollar, you make a dollar. That way you can grow your audience and it doesn't cost you any money."
- Fonte: `Issue 1 - Self Liquidating Content`
- Princípio: Content marketing costs should break even so audience growth is essentially free

**FIL-RB-FU-002** | Commitment as Life Pattern
> "We either make commitments or we don't, and today you made a commitment to jump in here and to join. Whatever you do in parts of your life, you're gonna be doing it in all parts of your life."
- Fonte: `transcription_Welcome_To_Funnel_University.txt`
- Aplicação: Member retention framing — people who cancel subscriptions have a life pattern of not committing

**FIL-RB-FU-003** | Value-First Selling (Chet Holmes Model)
> "Come in, put your credit card down but you don't have to pay anything right now. You go through the entire process, the entire three hours, and at the end of it, if you loved it, you would pay. If not, you didn't pay anything. Like going to a restaurant, eating all the food, and after you were done, if you thought it was amazing, then you would pay."
- Fonte: `transcription_Funnel_1_-_INVISIBLE_Funnel.txt`
- Princípio: Eliminate purchase risk by delivering value before payment — originated with Chet Holmes

**FIL-RB-FU-004** | Repel to Attract High-Ticket Clients
> "A lot of times we try to create things that are going to appeal to everyone and bring everybody in. Whereas Garrett's funnel is the opposite — all about chopping people out and pushing away so you only get the core few people that you do want."
- Fonte: `transcription_Funnel_2_-_High_Ticket_Funnel.txt` (Garrett White / Warrior Week)
- Princípio: High-ticket funnels gain strength by aggressively excluding wrong prospects

## L2 - MENTAL MODELS (5)

**MM-RB-FU-001** | Dream 100 x Content Strategy (Mike Dillard Model)
> "He interviews people on his Dream 100 list — people who are awesome AND have big audiences. After he interviews Drew Canole (2.5 million Facebook fans), he creates a page with Drew's picture saying 'Click here to listen to Drew's podcast.' Drew also sends it to his millions of followers. The interview is the front door to Mike's list."
- Fonte: `Issue 1 - Funnel University` + `transcription_Funnel_1_-_Self_Liquidating_Content.txt`
- Modelo: Content interviews serve dual purpose — create content AND access audience pools through audience swap

**MM-RB-FU-002** | Two Traffic Temperature Model
> "With warm traffic you can drive people to videos or webinars, all sorts of stuff. But a lot of times when you go into that cold, cold, cold traffic, people don't know who you are, they're scared. So this is a way to bring somebody into your world in a non-threatening way and get them warmed up."
- Fonte: `transcription_Funnel_2_-_REO_Rockstar_s_VSL.txt`
- Modelo: Cold → advertorial/pre-sell page; Warm → squeeze page or VSL directly. Same product, two entry points based on traffic temperature.

**MM-RB-FU-003** | Library vs. Splinter Mental Model
> "They take this library with tons and tons of stuff and they pull out each piece. Each sales page targets a specific demographic. Taking the analogy of a library — if I want to attract more business people, I find the best business book in my library, pull it out, and just sell that for really, really cheap. That way I get my dream clients to raise their hands."
- Fonte: `Issue 3 - Funnel University` (Digital Marketer's Continuity)
- Modelo: Membership = library; each piece of content = a book; sell individual books at low price to attract specific market segments to the main library

**MM-RB-FU-004** | Invisible Funnel Risk Reversal Psychology
> "The key to the Invisible Funnel is to deliver a tangible result to your audience. For Double Your Reading, Howard helps you double your reading speed during a four hour training session. After the training, you pay if and only if you double your reading speed."
- Fonte: `Issue 2 - Funnel University`
- Modelo: Remove ALL purchase risk by demanding payment only AFTER a specific tangible result is delivered — show-up rates and conversions dramatically increase

**MM-RB-FU-005** | Documentary vs. Lead Magnet for High-Ticket
> "Garrett said: 'I'm a man. What would I want? I like watching documentaries and videos and movies — that's what I want.' A lot of times we see tons of squeeze pages with 'hey, get a free ebook or free report.' Something stupid like that. But high-ticket clients want to watch a documentary and feel called out."
- Fonte: `transcription_Funnel_2_-_High_Ticket_Funnel.txt`
- Modelo: For high-ticket audiences, replace lead magnet with documentary video — they respond to immersive storytelling, not PDFs

## L3 - HEURISTICS (6)

**HEUR-RB-FU-001** | Invisible Funnel Show-Up Rate
> "We typically have 85% to 90% of registrants show up on these webinars and people stay for the full four hours."
- Fonte: `Issue 2 - Funnel University`
- Benchmark: 85-90% show-up rate (vs typical webinar 30-50%) due to payment obligation psychology

**HEUR-RB-FU-002** | Invisible Funnel Prepay Rate
> "Between 70% and 80% of people took the prepay option, which was amazing, because we ended up making a ton of money up front on what is basically a free offer!"
- Fonte: `Issue 2 - Funnel University`
- Benchmark: 70-80% choose to prepay at a discount vs keeping the free option — anchors them before the event

**HEUR-RB-FU-003** | Invisible Funnel Upsell Conversion
> "Howard has a whole bunch of other products and services — so we took all of his best stuff and bundled it together in one upsell. We offer his 12-week course for $197. The conversion on that was upwards of 18%."
- Fonte: `Issue 2 - Funnel University`
- Benchmark: ~18% upsell conversion after delivering a tangible result via Invisible Funnel

**HEUR-RB-FU-004** | Self-Liquidating Content Break-Even Target
> "If you spend a dollar, you make a dollar. That way you can grow your audience and it doesn't cost you any money."
- Fonte: `Issue 1 - Funnel University`
- Heurística: $1 in = $1 out minimum on front-end content promotion. Any profit comes from backend continuity.

**HEUR-RB-FU-005** | Splinter Product Pricing for List Building
> "There are some people who want cheap traffic, so the $10 A Day Traffic Plan will get them to raise their hand. What they do is just pull one piece from the library and sell it for $7 to $10."
- Fonte: `Issue 3 - Funnel University`
- Heurística: Price splinter products at $7-$10 — low enough to create impulse buyers, high enough to attract buyers (not freebie seekers)

**HEUR-RB-FU-006** | Continuity Self-Liquidation via High-Ticket Upsell
> "The goal of this $400 upsell offer is to cover all the ad costs to acquire front-end customers and hopefully make a little bit of a profit. After that, any monthly income from continuity is additional money in your pocket."
- Fonte: `Issue 3 - Funnel University` (Digital Marketer Continuity Model)
- Heurística: High-ticket upsell ($395-$400) should cover all ad costs — continuity revenue then becomes pure profit margin

## L4 - FRAMEWORKS (5)

**FW-RB-FU-001** | Self-Liquidating Content Funnel (Mike Dillard / Self Made Man)
```
STEP 1: Choose ONE content channel (blog/podcast/video)
STEP 2: Build Dream 100 list of people with large audiences in adjacent niches
STEP 3: Interview Dream 100 guests → create pages with their photos
STEP 4: Cross-promote — guest shares to their audience
STEP 5: Audience subscribes → leads to email list
STEP 6: Thank you page → membership pitch ($47)
GOAL: Self-liquidate ad spend through membership conversions
```
- Fonte: `Issue 1 - Funnel University` + `transcription_Funnel_1_-_Self_Liquidating_Content.txt`

**FW-RB-FU-002** | Invisible Funnel Framework (3-Page)
```
PAGE 1: Sales page — "pay only if you get the promised result"
        → Option A: Free (credit card on file, billed after if satisfied)
        → Option B: Prepay $X and save $20 (70-80% choose this)
PAGE 2: Upsell — bundle of related products at $197
PAGE 3: Members area — deliver the promised tangible result
POST-RESULT: If result delivered → bill. If not → honor promise, no charge.
BONUS: After delivering result, offer high-ticket program (best time to sell)
```
- Fonte: `Issue 2 - Funnel University` + `transcription_Funnel_1_-_INVISIBLE_Funnel.txt`
- Creditos: Original concept from Chet Holmes, first online application by Daegan Smith

**FW-RB-FU-003** | High-Ticket Repel Funnel (Garrett White / Warrior Week)
```
STEP 1: Specific ICP call-out in headline ("married businessmen" — not everyone)
STEP 2: Documentary video — intense, polarizing, calls out who you are NOT for
STEP 3: Application process — prospect must qualify
STEP 4: 1-on-1 close via phone/application
KEY PRINCIPLE: Aggressively cut out wrong people → right people feel MORE called to apply
```
- Fonte: `transcription_Funnel_2_-_High_Ticket_Funnel.txt` + `Issue 2 - Funnel University`

**FW-RB-FU-004** | Digital Marketer Continuity Multi-Entry Framework
```
CORE: Membership site = library with all content ($38.60/month)
ENTRIES: Take each piece of the library → sell as standalone at $7-$10
         Each entry point targets a DIFFERENT market segment
UPSELL 1: "$1 trial — you just got one piece, here's the whole library"
           → Converts to monthly continuity ($38.60/month)
UPSELL 2: Certification/high-ticket course at $395+
           → Self-liquidates ad costs
RESULT: From a few hundred to 10,000+ members via multiple entry points
```
- Fonte: `Issue 3 - Funnel University` + `transcription_Funnel_1_-_Digital_Marketer_s_Continuity_Funnel.txt`

**FW-RB-FU-005** | Political Bridge / Quiz Funnel (Cold Traffic Monetization)
```
STEP 1: Build political fan page (candidate-themed) on Facebook
STEP 2: Drive cold traffic to political quiz funnel
STEP 3: Quiz captures email → enters political email list
STEP 4: Bridge page transitions from political interest to product offer
STEP 5: Monetize list via ads and related offers
USE CASE: Converting political/ideological audience into buyers via bridge page
```

## L5 - METHODOLOGIES (4)

**MET-RB-FU-001** | Invisible Funnel Implementation
```
FASE 1 — DESIGN DO RESULTADO TANGÍVEL
  - Identificar resultado mensurável que pode ser entregue em 2-4 horas
  - Exemplo: "Double your reading speed" — testável e específico
  - Resultado deve ser verificável pelo participante

FASE 2 — CONSTRUÇÃO DA PÁGINA DE VENDAS
  - Headline: Big result + "or you don't pay"
  - Video: Script do Invisible Funnel (DotCom Secrets Section 4)
  - Abaixo do vídeo: Termos claros em 3 passos
    Step 1: Enter credit card
    Step 2: Attend workshop
    Step 3: Pay $X if you got the result / nothing if not
  - Order block: opção A (free) + opção B (prepay $X, save $20)

FASE 3 — UPSELL
  - Bundle do melhor conteúdo relacionado do especialista
  - Preço: ~$197
  - Momento: imediatamente após registro

FASE 4 — ENTREGA E COBRANÇA
  - Members area: handouts + replay + instrução do webinar
  - Cobrar automaticamente aqueles que não pediram cancelamento
  - Honrar cancelamentos sem questionar
```
- Fonte: `Issue 2 - Funnel University`

**MET-RB-FU-002** | Continuity Splinter Strategy Implementation
```
FASE 1 — MAPEAR A BIBLIOTECA
  - Listar todos os produtos/conteúdos dentro do membership
  - Identificar ângulos de mercado de cada um

FASE 2 — CRIAR SPLINTERS
  - Para cada produto: criar sales page standalone
  - Preço: $7-$10 por splinter
  - Cada página fala para UM segmento específico

FASE 3 — UPSELL PARA MEMBERSHIP
  - Após compra do splinter: "$1 trial — get all of it"
  - Mostrar tudo que eles estão perdendo ao comprar só uma peça
  - Converter para continuidade mensal

FASE 4 — UPSELL DE ALTO TICKET
  - Certificação ou programa avançado ($395+)
  - Meta: cobrir 100% do custo de aquisição com este upsell
  - Continuidade = lucro líquido

FASE 5 — MULTIPLICAR ENTRADAS
  - Repetir o processo para cada produto do membership
  - Resultado: dezenas de funis paralelos, cada um atraindo segmento diferente
```
- Fonte: `Issue 3 - Funnel University`

**MET-RB-FU-003** | Self-Liquidating Content System
```
FASE 1 — ESCOLHER CANAL
  - Um único canal (blog, podcast, vídeo, Periscope)
  - Consistência > variedade no início

FASE 2 — DREAM 100 PARA ENTREVISTAS
  - Identificar 100 pessoas: (a) awesome + (b) grande audiência
  - Selecionar prioridade: maior audiência + maior overlap de ICP

FASE 3 — PUBLICAÇÃO E CROSS-PROMOÇÃO
  - Criar página de landing com foto do entrevistado
  - Entrevistado divulga para audiência → audiência se inscreve no podcast/blog
  - Lead magnet: a entrevista em si

FASE 4 — MONETIZAÇÃO
  - Blog: formulário de optin abaixo do player
  - Thank you page: vídeo de vendas de membership ($47)
  - Objetivo: break-even em ads com conversão em membership
```
- Fonte: `Issue 1 - Funnel University`

**MET-RB-FU-004** | Cold Traffic Bridge Implementation
```
FASE 1 — ADVERTORIAL (para tráfego 100% frio)
  - Criar artigo que parece editorial (sem aspecto de anúncio)
  - Tema: tangencialmente relacionado ao produto principal
  - Exemplo: "Exposed loophole: how house flippers make money with zero out of pocket"
  - Objetivo: aquecer/pre-sell antes da VSL

FASE 2 — SPLIT DE TRÁFEGO
  - Tráfego frio → advertorial → VSL
  - Tráfego morno → squeeze page diretamente
  - Testar ambos com diferentes criativos

FASE 3 — VSL / SQUEEZE PAGE
  - Para frio: advertorial elimina objeção "quem é você?" antes da VSL
  - Para morno: squeeze com visual forte (flashing arrows) + optin simples

FASE 4 — BACK-END COMUM
  - Ambos os fluxos levam ao mesmo produto/oferta principal
```
- Fonte: `transcription_Funnel_2_-_REO_Rockstar_s_VSL.txt`
