## MODELOS MENTAIS (L2)

### [MM-RB-FU-101] — The Ruler Frame: Control What You're Judged By
> "Everyone's measuring you all the time. I don't want to have them judge me based on just my presentation skills alone... I want them to judge me based on what I'm promising."
^[RAIZ:/knowledge/sources/russell-brunson/raw/17._Funnel_University_2016/3. Sales Scripts/1. Perfect Webinar/transcription_Perfect_Webinar.txt]
**Domínio:** apresentação, frame, posicionamento
At the start of a webinar, declare the bold promise as the "ruler" by which the audience should evaluate the entire session. This reframes the measurement criteria away from delivery style toward outcome delivery.

---

### [MM-RB-FU-102] — The Prolific Zone: Between Mainstream and Crazy
> "In the prolific zone this is where you make a crap ton of money. The less sense you make on your registration page and the more curiosity based you are the more people are gonna opt in."
^[RAIZ:/knowledge/sources/russell-brunson/raw/17._Funnel_University_2016/3. Sales Scripts/2. Star, Story, Solution/transcription_Star__Story__Solution.txt]
**Domínio:** posicionamento, copywriting, oferta
There's a spectrum from mainstream (zero money, no curiosity) to crazy (alienating). The prolific zone sits between — offering ideas that are surprising and non-obvious but still believable. Dave Asprey's "butter in coffee" is a classic example.

---

### [MM-RB-FU-103] — Towards Pleasure vs. Away from Pain (Dual If-All Statements)
> "Most entrepreneurs make decisions moving towards pleasure... But the masses make their decisions moving away from pain. So when I do my if-all statements I am doing both of these."
^[RAIZ:/knowledge/sources/russell-brunson/raw/17._Funnel_University_2016/3. Sales Scripts/2. Star, Story, Solution/transcription_Star__Story__Solution.txt]
**Domínio:** copywriting, psicologia, fechamento
Entrepreneurs who sell to mass markets often fail because they only use pleasure-based closes that resonate with them — not with their buyers. Effective if-all statements alternate: some towards pleasure, some away from pain.

---

### [MM-RB-FU-104] — Curiosity Gap as the Core Registration Driver
> "Curiosity and pain — those are the two things. Whatever my one thing is, it has to be something they cannot figure out in their mind on their own."
^[RAIZ:/knowledge/sources/russell-brunson/raw/17._Funnel_University_2016/3. Sales Scripts/6. Perfect Webinar Funnel/transcription_Perfect_Webinar_Funnel.txt]
**Domínio:** páginas de registro, webinars, headlines
If the headline reveals the answer, people don't register (they think they already know). The headline must produce an open loop in the mind that can only be closed by attending. "My weird niche funnel making $17k/day and how to ethically knock it off in 10 minutes" is curiosity, not clarity.

### [MM-RB-FU-105] — One Sentence Persuasion: Five Emotional Levers
> "People will do anything for those who encourage their dreams, justify their failures, allay their fears, confirm their suspicions, and help them throw rocks at their enemies."
^[RAIZ:/knowledge/sources/russell-brunson/raw/17._Funnel_University_2016/3. Sales Scripts/2. Star, Story, Solution/transcription_Star__Story__Solution.txt]

Sourced from Blair Warren's "One Sentence Persuasion Course." The entire star-story-solution arc is designed to execute all five levers: backstory encourages dreams, conspiracy justifies failure, common enemy gives rocks to throw.

### [MM-RB-FU-106] — Three False Belief Patterns That Block Every Sale
> "What are the three false belief patterns that are keeping them currently from giving me money? I've got to show them that their belief pattern is wrong, I've got to break it, and then rebuild it."
^[RAIZ:/knowledge/sources/russell-brunson/raw/17._Funnel_University_2016/3. Sales Scripts/6. Perfect Webinar Funnel/transcription_Perfect_Webinar_Funnel.txt]
**Domínio:** webinars, estrutura de conteúdo, crenças
The three secrets in a webinar are NOT random teaching points — they are carefully selected to demolish the three specific false beliefs blocking the purchase. In most markets these cluster around: (1) it's too hard, (2) it takes too much time, (3) I can't get traffic/results.

### [FW-RB-FU-106] — Webinar Registration Page Anatomy
> "The only copy on my landing page is basically: here's my one thing — and down here are my three secrets. The headline must produce curiosity they cannot resolve in their own head."
^[RAIZ:/knowledge/sources/russell-brunson/raw/17._Funnel_University_2016/3. Sales Scripts/6. Perfect Webinar Funnel/transcription_Perfect_Webinar_Funnel.txt]
**Domínio:** páginas de registro, copywriting, funil
Anatomy: **(1) Curiosity-gap headline** (how to [thing they want] without [thing they hate] — wrapped in something they cannot Google-answer), **(2) One thing** clearly stated, **(3) Three secrets previewed** (teased, not revealed), **(4) Weird/curious image** that raises more questions than it answers, **(5) Simple opt-in form.** Tested rule: the less sensible the page looks + the more curiosity-based = higher opt-in rate across all split tests.

## METODOLOGIAS (L5)

### [MET-RB-FU-101] — The Stack Method (Armand Morin)
> "He said: most presenters go through all the stuff they're gonna get and in the last slide they're like 'this is bonus number 12' — and the only thing the human mind remembers is the last thing they were shown. So I invented the stack."
^[RAIZ:/knowledge/sources/russell-brunson/raw/17._Funnel_University_2016/3. Sales Scripts/1. Perfect Webinar/transcription_Perfect_Webinar.txt]
**Domínio:** fechamento, webinars, apresentações
**Step-by-step:** (1) Show component 1 → state value → restate full running total. (2) Add component 2 → new value → restate EVERYTHING again. (3) Keep restacking after each component and after each close. (4) After all components, show big total price. (5) Do not apologize for big price — instead justify it with If-All statements (3+ statements, mixing toward-pleasure and away-from-pain). (6) Price drop reveal. (7) CTA on every slide from this point forward. **Key rule:** Never say "don't worry I won't charge you that" without first justifying the big price. The stack has generated $10M+ in direct attribution for Brunson. First use at Armand's event: ~$100K sold, became #1 salesperson.

### [MET-RB-FU-102] — Writing Copy in Two-Brain Mode
> "There's two parts of your brain — left brain and right brain. When you're writing copy if you're using both sides of your brain you'll never get good copy. Just write as fast as you can. Don't stop for typos, don't do punctuation. Or open iPhone recorder, talk your way through it, send to Fiverr for transcription."
^[RAIZ:/knowledge/sources/russell-brunson/raw/17._Funnel_University_2016/3. Sales Scripts/2. Star, Story, Solution/transcription_Star__Story__Solution.txt]
**Domínio:** copywriting, processo de criação
**Step-by-step:** (1) Open template with all section headers (Pattern Interrupt, Core Desire Questions, Agitate Past Failures, etc.). (2) Close eyes and write/talk as fast as possible — zero editing. (3) If writing, use plain text editor (Notepad/TextMate) to strip formatting temptation. (4) Alternatively: record iPhone voice memo, upload to Fiverr for $5 transcription. (5) ONLY after full draft is done: switch to analytical brain to edit, clean, and sequence. This two-phase process protects creative output from internal censorship.

### [MET-RB-FU-103] — Asking Permission to Sell (Transition Into Close)
> "I always ask permission before I sell because as soon as they said 'yes, you can sell to me,' I just changed everything. 'Do you guys mind if I take a minute and share what's inside Funnel Hacks?' And I wait. And it's always awkward. But you've got to be okay with awkward."
^[RAIZ:/knowledge/sources/russell-brunson/raw/17._Funnel_University_2016/3. Sales Scripts/6. Perfect Webinar Funnel/transcription_Perfect_Webinar_Funnel.txt]
**Domínio:** fechamento, transição, webinars
**Step-by-step:** (1) Finish content section. (2) Ask an open question: "Let me ask you a question — how many of you would be interested if I had a way for you to continue this conversation?" (3) Wait for affirmation in chat. (4) Ask explicit permission: "Are you okay if I take a minute and share what's inside [product]?" (5) Wait in silence — do not fill the silence. (6) Once they say yes → begin the stack. Sourced from Joel Bauer. Russell calls this the most critical transition in the webinar.
