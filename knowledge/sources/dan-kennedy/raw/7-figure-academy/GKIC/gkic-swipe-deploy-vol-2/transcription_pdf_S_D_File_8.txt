Over the past twenty years, 18% of the population moves every year (staggering statistic). What's
more, 'just-moved-in' families have a powerful need, both rational and emotional, to replace
previously familiar 'connections' - such as favorite places to eat, bank, fix their cars, work out,
shop, etc.
f»
These modern nomads are motivated, both positively and negatively, to make new friends, build
new loyalties and establish new buying habits. In fact, experts find new residents twice as loyal
to their "new connections."
The bottom-line effect of all of this is significant. Research suggests that on average, new
neighbors spend 140% more than established residents and they are far more loyal, sticking with
their "first picks" more than 210% longer than new customers acquired through other sources.
What I have found works best in order to attract these New Homeowners is the following:
• A targeted multi-step campaign
• A FREE 'no-strings attached' offer

Response Boosters:
Response Booster #1: The LIST
It amazes me what's available in terms of selecting the correct list that most matches up with
your existing customers. The important thing is NOT to mail to all new Movers, but to only mail to
new movers who most closely resemble existing customers. A good list broker (or better yet, our
recommended resource) can help you identify those new homeowners who most closely match
your best prospects so you can avoid wasting resources on unlikely prospects.
Response Booster #2: When to Mail
Now this needs to be tested, but I suggest you start with mailing to New Homeowners the 3rd
month after they have settled into their homes, as they tend to be inundated with mailings their
1st and 2nd month. The good news is that the mailings they're inundated with early are typically
the co-op format where your offer is combined with other businesses in your area. This robs you
of any individuality and the ability to select exactly the names that best match your existing
customers-
Response Booster #3: ONLY mail to New Homeowners Again, this can be tested, but typically
response rates are much better with Homeowners vs. Renters. Of course, some businesses (such
as Restaurants and Auto Repair) might do fine with Apartment Dwellers.
