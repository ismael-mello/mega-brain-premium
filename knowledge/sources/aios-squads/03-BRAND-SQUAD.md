## KEY FRAMEWORKS BY CATEGORY

### Positioning Frameworks

- **22 Immutable Laws of Branding** (Ries): Leadership, Category, Mind, Perception, Focus, Exclusivity, Ladder, Duality, Opposite, Division, Perspective, Line Extension, Sacrifice, Attributes, Candor, Singularity, Unpredictability, Success, Failure, Hype, Acceleration, Resources
- **Onlyness Statement** (Neumeier): "Our [offering] is the only [category] that [differentiation]"
- **Category Creation** (Ries): If not first in category, create new category to be first
- **Mad Libs Positioning** (Heyward): For [target] who [need], [product] is [category] that [emotional] unlike [alternatives]

### Brand Equity Frameworks

- **Aaker Brand Equity Model** (5 pillars): Awareness, Perceived Quality, Associations, Loyalty, Proprietary Assets
- **CBBE Pyramid** (Keller, 4 layers): Identity ("Who are you?" - Salience) → Meaning ("What are you?" - Performance + Imagery) → Response ("What do I think/feel?" - Judgments + Feelings) → Resonance ("What connection?" - Loyalty + Attachment + Community + Engagement)
- **Brand Value Chain** (Keller): Investment → Mindset → Performance → Shareholder Value
- **Brand Vision Model** (Aaker, 4 perspectives): Product, Organization, Person, Symbol

### Brand Identity Frameworks

- **Identity Prism** (Kapferer, 6 facets): Physique, Personality, Culture, Relationship, Reflection, Self-Image
- **9 Brand Identity Ideals** (Wheeler): Vision, Meaning, Authenticity, Differentiation, Sustainability, Coherence, Flexibility, Commitment, Value
- **Mark Types** (Wheeler): Wordmarks, Letterform, Pictorial, Abstract, Emblems, Dynamic, Characters

### Brand Messaging Frameworks

- **StoryBrand SB7** (Miller, 7 elements): Character (customer=hero), Problem (3 levels: external, internal, philosophical), Guide (brand=empathy+authority), Plan (3 steps), CTA (direct+transitional), Failure (stakes), Success (transformation)
- **One-Liner** (Miller): [Problem] + [Solution] + [Result] in 25 words or less
- **Grunt Test** (Miller): Can a caveman understand what you do in 5 seconds?

### Growth & Measurement Frameworks

- **Mental + Physical Availability** (Sharp): Only two true brand growth drivers
- **Double Jeopardy Law** (Sharp): Smaller brands = fewer buyers + less loyal buyers
- **Reach > Frequency** (Sharp): Reach more people once > few people many times
- **Category Entry Points** (Sharp): Internal cues that trigger category thinking

### Brand Personality Frameworks

- **12 Jungian Archetypes**: Innocent, Explorer, Sage, Hero, Outlaw, Magician, Everyman, Lover, Jester, Caregiver, Creator, Ruler
- **Aaker's 5 Personality Dimensions**: Sincerity, Excitement, Competence, Sophistication, Ruggedness

### Brand Culture Frameworks

- **FUSION** (Yohn): Brand + Culture = organizational power
- **7 Brand-Building Principles** (Yohn): Start Inside, Avoid Selling Products, Ignore Trends, Don't Chase Customers, Sweat Small Stuff, Commit and Stay, Never Give Back
- **9 Brand Types** (Yohn): Disruptive, Conscious, Service, Innovative, Value, Performance, Luxury, Style, Experience

### Visual Identity Frameworks

- **5-Phase Process** (Wheeler): Research → Strategy → Design → Touchpoints → Assets

### Startup Branding Frameworks

- **Why Test** (Heyward): Keep asking "why" until reaching fear of death
- **Brand from Day One** (Heyward): Build brand before product-market fit, not after

### Naming Frameworks

- **8-Type Taxonomy**: Descriptive, Suggestive, Abstract, Acronymic, Founder, Metaphorical, Compound, Altered
- **Phonosemantics**: Plosives=strength, Fricatives=speed, Nasals=warmth, Front vowels=precision, Back vowels=power
- **8 Evaluation Criteria**: Memorability, Pronounceability, Spellability, Distinctiveness, Meaning, Scalability, Protectability, Domain

## HEURISTICS (40+ Rules)

### Positioning Rules

- Better to be first than to be better (Ries)
- Own one word in prospect's mind (Ries)
- Strategy from marketplace, not boardroom (Ries)
- When you try to be everything, you end up being nothing (Ries)
- For every category, there are only two viable brands (Ries - Law of Duality)
- Narrow focus beats broad scope (Ries - Law of Sacrifice)
- The essence of a brand is a singular idea (Ries - Law of Singularity)
- Line extension destroys brand equity (Ries)

### Identity & Equity Rules

- Brand is a person's gut feeling about a product (Neumeier)
- Your brand isn't what you say it is; it's what THEY say it is (Neumeier)
- Brand is asset, not expense (Aaker)
- Identity precedes image (Kapferer)
- Luxury brand grows by maintaining rarity, not chasing volume (Kapferer)
- Brand salience trumps brand preference (Keller)
- Building equity requires consistency over time (Aaker)
- Portfolio brands must have clear roles (Aaker)

### Growth Rules

- Grow by penetration, not loyalty (Sharp)
- Light buyers are the growth engine (Sharp)
- Continuous advertising > pulsed advertising (Sharp)
- Distinctiveness > differentiation (Sharp)
- All brands share most of their buyers with competitors (Sharp)
- Market-based assets matter more than perceived differentiation (Sharp)
- Physical availability drives more sales than emotional connection (Sharp)

### Messaging Rules

- If you confuse, you lose (Miller)
- Customer is the hero, brand is the guide (Miller)
- People buy solutions to INTERNAL problems (Miller)
- Pretty websites don't sell things; words sell things (Miller)
- Show empathy first, then authority (Miller)
- Always include a clear call to action (Miller)
- Define what's at stake if customer doesn't act (Miller)

### Culture Rules

- Great brands start INSIDE the organization (Yohn)
- Your brand is what you DO, not what you SAY (Yohn)
- All the little things you DO > big things you SAY (Yohn)
- Culture and brand must be aligned or both suffer (Yohn)

### Startup Rules

- Brand matters from BEFORE day one (Heyward)
- Data can't tell you brand positioning (Heyward)
- Familiar problem, unexpected answer (Heyward)
- Community is brand's most powerful asset early on (Heyward)

### Naming Rules

- Name = strategic asset, not creative accident
- Sound carries measurable meaning (phonosemantics)
- Name you can't protect = name you don't own
- Generate 30-50 candidates before selecting
- Test pronounceability across target languages

## METHODOLOGIES (9 Operational Systems)

1. **Brand Audit** - 4-phase assessment: Equity audit + Identity audit + Positioning audit + Touchpoint audit
2. **Create Positioning** - Category analysis → Strategy selection → Statement crafting → Activation plan
3. **Build Identity** - Personality mapping → Visual system → Verbal system → Brand guidelines
4. **Create Brand Story** - SB7 BrandScript → One-liner → Full narrative → Application across touchpoints
5. **Design Architecture** - Model selection (branded house/house of brands/endorsed/hybrid) → Design → Role assignment → Governance
6. **Generate Names** - Strategy brief → Generation (30-50 candidates) → 8-criteria evaluation → Shortlist (3-5)
7. **Map Archetype** - Brand analysis → Archetype identification → Expression guide → Messaging applications
8. **Diagnose Challenge** - Rapid triage (symptoms → root cause) → Route to specialist expert
9. **Review Output** - Quality gate with 3 outcomes: approve, revise with notes, reject with rationale
